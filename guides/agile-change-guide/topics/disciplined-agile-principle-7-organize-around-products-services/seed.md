# Disciplined Agile principle 7: Organize around products/services

**Organize around products/services: Structure teams and work around value streams (the products and services that deliver value) rather than traditional functional silos.**

"Organize around products/services" is a shift away from traditional "project-based" or functional department thinking. Instead of bringing people to the work (moving tasks from department to department), you bring the work to stable, long-lived teams.

Here is how this principle changes the game:

- From Projects to Products: Projects are temporary and end when the "budget" is gone. Products are ongoing. By focusing on products, teams stay together, build deep domain expertise, and take long-term responsibility for the quality of what they build.
- Cross-Functional Teams: Instead of having a "Testing Department" and a "Development Department," you build a team that has everyone needed to deliver value—developers, testers, designers, and business experts—all working on the same product.
- Value Streams: It encourages the organization to identify its Value Streams (the end-to-end series of actions that deliver a reward to a customer) and align the people directly to those streams.
- Reduced Hand-offs: When people are organized around a product, you eliminate the "waiting time" that happens when one department has to hand work over to another.

By organizing this way, you reduce complexity and ensure that everyone is focused on the same outcome: Delighting the Customer.
