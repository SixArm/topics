# Disciplined Agile principle 7: Organize around products/services

**Organize around products/services: Structure teams and work around value
streams (the products and services that deliver value) rather than traditional
functional silos.**

Organizations should build stable, long-lived teams aligned directly to the
products and services that deliver value to customers.

Teams stay together, accumulate deep domain knowledge, and take ongoing
responsibility for the quality and evolution of what they deliver. This
continuity leads to better architectural decisions, faster onboarding, and
stronger ownership.

The principle demands cross-functional teams. Rather than maintaining separate
testing, development, and design departments, each product team includes
everyone needed to deliver value: developers, testers, designers, business
analysts, and domain experts. This eliminates handoffs and wait times that
accumulate when work moves from silo to silo. A feature that once required
coordination across four departments can move from concept to delivery within a
single team.

The practical result is reduced complexity. Teams spend less time coordinating
across organizational boundaries and more time building. Feedback loops shorten
because the people who design, build, test, and support a product sit together
and share context continuously. Accountability becomes clear: one team owns one
product, and success or failure is unambiguous.
