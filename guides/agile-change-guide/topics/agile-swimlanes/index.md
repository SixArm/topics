# Agile swimlanges

Agile swimlanes are horizontal rows on a task board that categorize work items by a shared attribute. While the board's vertical columns represent workflow stages such as "To Do," "In Progress," and "Done," swimlanes add a second dimension that helps teams organize parallel workstreams and prioritize effectively.

Teams customize swimlanes based on what they need to visualize most clearly. Common configurations include an expedite lane at the top of the board for critical unplanned work like production outages, lanes per team member to balance individual workloads, lanes per project or client for teams managing multiple deliverables simultaneously, lanes per epic to track how high-level goals are progressing through their subtasks, and lanes by class of service to distinguish work by its cost of delay.

Swimlanes provide several practical benefits. They expose bottlenecks by making it immediately visible when one category of work is consistently stalling. They clarify accountability by showing who owns what and where handoffs between roles occur. They support flow management by enabling teams to set work-in-progress limits on specific lanes, preventing overcommitment. They also reduce visual clutter on busy boards because lanes can be collapsed, letting the team focus on the most relevant slice of work at any given time.

The key design decision is choosing which dimension to visualize as swimlanes versus using other mechanisms like labels or filters. Pick the dimension that creates the most important at-a-glance insight for your team's daily standup and workflow decisions.
