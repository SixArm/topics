# Agile swimlanges

In Agile, swimlanes are horizontal rows on a task board to categorize work items. Vertical columns represent the status of a task (e.g., "Doable", "Doing", "Done"), swimlanes add a second dimension to help teams organize parallel workstreams and prioritize effectively. 

## Common Types of Swimlanes

Teams customize their swimlanes based on what they need to visualize most clearly:

- Expedite (Urgency): A single lane at the very top for critical, unplanned work (like a production outage) that needs to bypass the normal workflow. 

- Assignee: One row per team member to show individual workloads and ensure no one is over-capacity. 

- Project or Client: Ideal for agencies or teams managing deliverables for multiple external clients or internal departments on one board. 

- Epics or Stories: Grouping small sub-tasks under their larger parent "Epic" to see how high-level goals are progressing. 

- Classes of Service: Categorizing work by its "cost of delay," such as fixed-date requirements vs. standard maintenance. 

## Key Benefits

- Visualizes Bottlenecks: By separating work into lanes, teams can quickly see if one department or project is consistently lagging. 

- Clarifies Accountability: It makes it "crystal clear" who owns what and where handoffs between different roles happen. 

- Manages Flow: Swimlanes allow teams to set WIP (Work in Progress) limits for specific categories, ensuring they don't start more than they can finish. 

- Reduces Clutter: On busy boards, swimlanes can often be collapsed, allowing teams to focus on just the active project or high-priority lane.
