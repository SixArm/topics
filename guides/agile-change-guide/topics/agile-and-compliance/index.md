# Agile and compliance

In Agile development, compliance is often viewed as a gate at the end of a project, but successful Agile teams treat it as a continuous design constraint integrated into every sprint. The challenge is that traditional compliance relies on fixed requirements and static documentation, which clashes with Agile's iterative nature. Common friction points include documentation gaps where auditors require detailed audit trails that minimal Agile documentation does not naturally produce, approval delays where sequential sign-off processes stall rapid release cycles, and terminology gaps where engineers and legal teams define terms differently.

To balance speed with regulatory safety, organizations use several key strategies. Shifting compliance left means involving legal and risk experts early in planning and backlog refinement rather than treating them as external gatekeepers at the end. Embedding regulatory requirements into the team's Definition of Done ensures no feature is considered complete until it meets compliance standards. Compliance as code codifies policies into automated tests that run within CI/CD pipelines, providing real-time validation and generating audit trails automatically. Agile governance implements lightweight milestones and metrics to provide the oversight regulators expect without the heavy paperwork of waterfall methodologies.

The most effective teams reject the premise that agility and compliance are in tension. They recognize that continuous compliance actually reduces risk compared to batch-and-queue audits, because problems surface immediately rather than accumulating undetected across multiple sprints. When compliance is a first-class concern woven into daily work, it stops being a bottleneck and becomes a quality accelerator.
