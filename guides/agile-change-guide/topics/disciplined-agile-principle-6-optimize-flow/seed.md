# Disciplined Agile principle 6: Optimize flow

**Optimize flow: Focus on streamlining the end-to-end delivery of value across the entire organization, not just within a single team.**

"Optimize flow" is about ensuring that value moves from concept to the customer as quickly and smoothly as possible. It’s not just about how fast a developer writes code; it’s about the entire journey.

In Disciplined Agile, optimizing flow means:

- Looking at the Whole System: Don't just optimize one team if the work gets stuck for three weeks in "Testing" or "Legal." You want to eliminate bottlenecks across the entire Value Stream.
- Visualizing Work: Using tools like Kanban boards to see where work is "piling up" so the team can address the clog immediately.
- Reducing Waste: Identifying and removing things that don't add value, such as unnecessary documentation, long wait times, or "gold-plating" (adding features the customer didn't ask for).
- Limiting Work in Progress (WIP): Realizing that "starting" isn't the same as "finishing." By doing fewer things at once, teams actually get more things done.

By focusing on flow, you move from "being busy" to actually delivering results.
