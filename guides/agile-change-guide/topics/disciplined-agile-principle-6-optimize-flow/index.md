# Disciplined Agile principle 6: Optimize flow

**Optimize flow: Focus on streamlining the end-to-end delivery of value across the entire organization, not just within a single team.**

This principle shifts attention from local team efficiency to the smooth
movement of work from concept to customer. A development team might be highly
productive, but if work stalls for weeks in testing, legal review, or deployment
queues, that speed is wasted. The goal is to identify and eliminate bottlenecks
across the entire value stream so that value reaches customers without
unnecessary delays.

Limiting work in progress is one of the most effective flow strategies. Teams
that start many items simultaneously finish fewer of them. By constraining how
much work is active at any time, teams reduce context switching and deliver more
often.

Reducing waste is important. This means removing activities that do not
contribute to customer value, such as excessive documentation, redundant
handoffs, long approval wait times, and gold-plating features that nobody
requested.

Visualization is a helpful practice for examining flow. Techniques like Kanban
boards make work visible, revealing where items pile up and where capacity is
misaligned. When teams can see congestion forming, they can address it
immediately rather than discovering problems after deadlines slip.

Optimizing flow prioritizes throughput and cycle time over activity, ensuring
that organizations measure success by value delivered rather than effort
expended.
