# Disciplined Agile principle 1: Delight customers

**Delight customers: Strive to exceed both internal and external customer
expectations rather than just satisfying basic needs.**

Teams strive to exceed customer expectations rather than merely satisfy stated
requirements. This shifts the organizational mindset from fulfilling
specifications to creating genuine advantages through exceptional products and
services. From a competitive standpoint, the principle acknowledges a market
reality: adequate products lose to excellent ones.

Disciplined Agile broadens the definition of "customer" beyond the end user or
buyer. Internal stakeholders such as operations, security, and legal teams are
also customers whose needs must be anticipated and exceeded. A product that
delights end users but creates operational problems has not fulfilled this
principle. Teams practicing DA think holistically about everyone affected by
their work.

In practice, this principle drives adoption of the Disciplined Agile Exploratory
Lifecycle, which applies Lean Startup techniques to validate ideas quickly
through experimentation.
