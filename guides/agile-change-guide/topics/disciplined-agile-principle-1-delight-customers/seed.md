# Disciplined Agile principle 1: Delight customers

**Delight customers: Strive to exceed both internal and external customer expectations rather than just satisfying basic needs.**

Within the Disciplined Agile (DA) framework, Delighting Customers is the ultimate goal of any value-producing activity. It shifts the focus from simply "meeting requirements" to creating a competitive advantage through exceptional service and product quality.

In a DA context, this principle is put into practice by:

- Going Beyond Specifications: Realizing that customers often don't know exactly what they need until they see it, so teams must use feedback loops to discover what truly provides value.
- Internal & External Focus: Recognizing that a "customer" isn't just the person buying the software; it’s also the downstream team (like Operations or Legal) that relies on your output.
- Competitiveness: Understanding that in a modern economy, simply "satisfying" a user isn't enough to prevent them from switching to a competitor who offers a better experience.

This principle directly drives the Exploratory Lifecycle in DA, which uses Lean Startup techniques to test ideas quickly so you can pivot toward what actually delights the user.
