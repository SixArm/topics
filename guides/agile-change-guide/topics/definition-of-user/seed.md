# Definition of User (DoU)

A user is the person who ultimately uses or is intended to use the product to perform a task or achieve a goal. They are often distinguished from the customer, who pays for or purchases the product, and from the operators, such as administrators or maintenance staff who support the system. 

## Key Characteristics

- Primary Value Target: The ultimate goal of projects is to create value for the user.
- Operational Focus: Users typically have hands-on, daily interaction with the product to solve specific real-world problems.
- Non-Technical Perspective: They focus on usability and functionality rather than the underlying technical details or architectural assumptions made by developers.
- Feedback Source: Their feedback is considered the best indicator of a product's actual performance and real-world value. 

## Users in the Software Development Lifecycle

User Stories: Requirements are often written from a user's perspective (e.g., "As a persona...") to ensure features align with user goals. 

Continuous Feedback Loops: Users can provide input during sprint reviews and through surveys or direct communication to fuel ongoing improvements. 

Testing and Validation: Users can participate in user acceptance testing and usability studies to identify bugs or missing features that the development team might have overlooked. 

Co-development: In some teams, knowledgeable end users may work directly with the delivery team to help refine user stories and define acceptance criteria.
