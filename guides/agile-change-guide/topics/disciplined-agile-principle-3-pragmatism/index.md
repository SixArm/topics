# Disciplined Agile principle 3: Pragmatism

**Pragmatism: Focus on being effective rather than dogmatically "being agile".
This may involve using lean or even traditional strategies if they fit the
situation better.**

This principle serves as the reality check of the Disciplined Agile mindset,
pushing teams away from agile dogmatism and toward genuine effectiveness.

Context drives decisions under this principle. Every team operates within
different constraints, skill levels, regulatory environments, and organizational
cultures. Pragmatism means selecting the right approach for the situation at
hand rather than forcing a single framework where it does not fit.

Success matters more than ideological purity. If a traditional waterfall
technique or a specific lean practice is the most effective solution for a given
problem, use it.

This flexibility is not an excuse for sloppiness. Pragmatism in Disciplined
Agile means making intentional, disciplined choices backed by reasoning, not
abandoning rigor.

A clear example is Disciplined Agile's support for multiple lifecycles. Where
some frameworks insist on fixed-length sprints, some teams may thrive with
Continuous Delivery, or lean flow, or exploratory approaches depending on their
context and goals.
