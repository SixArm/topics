# Disciplined Agile promises

In Disciplined Agile, the Promises are the commitments made by team members to one another and to the rest of the organization. If the Principles are the "Why," the Promises are the "How" for behaving in a disciplined way.

There are 7 Promises in the DA mindset:

- Create Psychological Safety and Trust: This is the bedrock. You promise to create an environment where people can speak up, experiment, and fail without fear, knowing that trust is earned and must be maintained.
- Collaborate Proactively: You don't wait to be told to work with others. You reach out across team boundaries, share knowledge, and offer help whenever it's needed to move the mission forward.
- Make All Work and Workflow Visible: To optimize flow, everyone must see what is happening. You promise to use visual tools (like Kanban boards) so that bottlenecks and "hidden" tasks are exposed to everyone.
- Keep Workloads Within Capacity: Overburdened teams make mistakes and burn out. You promise to limit Work in Progress (WIP) and ensure that the team is not taking on more than it can realistically finish.
- Accelerate Value Realization: This is about speed to market. You promise to focus on finishing work that delivers value quickly, rather than just staying busy with low-priority tasks.
- Be Transparent: You promise to be honest about the state of the project—including risks, delays, and failures—so that the organization can make informed decisions based on reality, not "status report green" fluff.
- Improve Continuously: You promise to never be satisfied with the status quo. You will constantly look for ways to improve your Way of Working (WoW) using the DA toolkit to guide your experiments.

These promises turn the philosophy of the Principles into a social contract that holds the team together.
