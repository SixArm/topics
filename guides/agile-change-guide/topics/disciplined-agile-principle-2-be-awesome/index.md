# Disciplined Agile principle 2: Be awesome

**Be awesome: Build high-performing, motivated teams where individuals are
supported to be their best and continuously improve.**

Agile success depends not just on processes but on the humans executing them.
Motivated people who take pride in their craft produce higher quality results
and better business outcomes. Investing in team culture, individual growth, and
working conditions drives of sustainable high-quality delivery.

Being awesome means creating workplaces where people thrive. Organizations must
establish psychological safety so team members feel comfortable taking risks,
asking questions, admitting mistakes, and raising concerns. When people feel
safe, they experiment more freely and surface problems early, which directly
improves delivery outcomes.

The principle calls for developing T-shaped individuals who combine deep
expertise in one discipline with enough breadth to collaborate effectively
across the team. This skill diversity reduces bottlenecks, improves knowledge
sharing, and makes teams more resilient when priorities shift or members are
unavailable.

Disciplined Agile embraces the Kaizen mindset, giving teams both the time and
authority to experiment with their Way of Working and evolve their practices.
This principle drives Guided Continuous Improvement, to identify promising next
steps for team growth.
