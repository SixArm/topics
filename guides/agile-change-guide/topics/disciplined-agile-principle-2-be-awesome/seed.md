# Disciplined Agile principle 2: Be awesome

**Be awesome: Build high-performing, motivated teams where individuals are supported to be their best and continuously improve.**

Being Awesome is the "people-first" principle of Disciplined Agile. It recognizes that agile isn't just about processes—it's about the humans executing them.

In the DA toolkit, this principle is more than just a motivational slogan; it involves specific organizational commitments:

- Psychological Safety: Creating an environment where people feel safe to take risks, admit mistakes, and voice concerns without fear of retribution.

- Skill Diversity: Moving toward "T-shaped" individuals who have deep expertise in one area but enough broad knowledge to collaborate across the team.

- Continuous Improvement: Embracing the "Kaizen" mindset, where teams are given the time and authority to experiment with their Way of Working (WoW) to become more effective.

- Joy at Work: Believing that motivated people who take pride in their craft naturally produce higher quality work and better outcomes for the business.

This principle is the engine behind Guided Continuous Improvement (GCI)—the DA technique for helping teams get better faster by using the toolkit to identify the most likely "next steps" for growth.
