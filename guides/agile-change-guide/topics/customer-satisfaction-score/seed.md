# Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT)

Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT) is a key performance indicator (KPI) measuring how satisfied customers are with a specific product, service, or interaction using a 1–5 scale (very dissatisfied to very satisfied). It is calculated as: 

## Key Aspects of CSAT

Measurement: Usually asked immediately after an interaction (e.g., support call or purchase).

Interpretation: A higher percentage indicates higher satisfaction, with 100% being excellent.

Limitations: CSAT can suffer from low response rates (often ~5%), with respondents often being extremely happy or unhappy.

Purpose: Helps businesses monitor and improve customer experience to reduce churn. 

Common CSAT Questions:

- "How would you rate your overall satisfaction with the [goods/service] you received?"
- "How satisfied were you with the support you received?" 

How to Improve CSAT:

- Provide quick service: Reduce customer effort to resolve issues.
- Personalize interactions: Use customer data to tailor the experience.
- Act on feedback: Use, as discussed in, platforms like Medallia to monitor and act on feedback.
- Set clear expectations: Communicate clearly regarding product capabilities and support times. 

Difference from Other Metrics:
- CSAT: Measures short-term happiness with a specific interaction.
- NPS (Net Promoter Score): Measures long-term loyalty and likelihood to recommend.
- CES (Customer Effort Score): Measures how easy it was to use the service
