# Get out of the building

"Get out of the building" (GOOB) is a mantra coined by Steve Blank, the father of the Lean Startup movement. It challenges the dangerous assumption that you already know what your customers want. In his view, "there are no facts inside the building, only opinions".

To build a product people actually want, you must leave your office and observe potential users in their real-world environment to validate your business hypotheses.

## How GOOB Works with Agile

In a startup or new product team, Agile is the "how" (the iterative building process), while Customer Development (GOOB) is the "why" and "for whom".

The Partnership: Customer Development is "deaf, dumb, and blind" if the engineering team uses slow, rigid waterfall methods. Agile allows teams to take the insights gained from "outside the building" and quickly pivot or iterate the product.

The Goal: You aren't just gathering data or running focus groups. You are looking for insight—deeply understanding the customer's daily routine, pain points, and frustrations so you can build a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) that solves a real problem.

## The 4 Steps of Customer Development

Steve Blank's framework for "getting out of the building" involves four distinct phases:

- Customer Discovery: Founders test their initial hypotheses about customer problems and needs by talking to real people.
- Customer Validation: Proving the business model is scalable by seeing if customers will actually pay for the solution.
- Customer Creation: Scaling the company by driving end-user demand through marketing and sales.
- Company Building: Transitioning from a learning-focused startup to a formal organization designed for execution.
