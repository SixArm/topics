# Disciplined Agile principle 5: Choice is good

**Choice is Good: Understand that having a wide selection of tools and techniques allows for better, informed decisions.**

Teams perform best when they can select their own practices and processes rather
than following a single prescribed method. This principle builds directly on the
ideas that context counts and pragmatism matters: every team faces unique
circumstances, then offering only one way to work causes suboptimal fit for
most teams.

Traditional agile frameworks often mandate specific practices for every team.
They require a specific cadence, or fixed roles, or particular backlog format.

Disciplined Agile takes a fundamentally different approach by providing process
goals paired with multiple strategies for achieving them. When a team needs to
coordinate activities, for example, they can choose from daily stand-ups, kanban
boards, weekly status meetings, or other techniques based on what actually works
in their environment.

The Disciplined Agile toolkit addresses this by documenting the trade-offs
associated with each option. Teams can evaluate which practices fit their
current situation, team size, organizational constraints, and technical
landscape. This turns selection from an uninformed gamble into a deliberate,
informed decision.

Teams avoid what practitioners call "framework prison," where rigid adherence to
a single methodology prevents adaptation and improvement. Instead, teams evolve
their Way of Working over time as they gain experience and as their context
evolves. Choice keeps the process serving the team rather than the team serving
the process.
