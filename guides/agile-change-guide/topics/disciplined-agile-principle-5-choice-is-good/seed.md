# Disciplined Agile principle 5: Choice is good

**Choice is Good: Understand that having a wide selection of tools and techniques allows for better, informed decisions.**

"Choice is good" is the practical application of the previous two principles: Because "Context Counts" and you want to be "Pragmatic," you need a wide variety of options to choose from.

In many frameworks, you are given a single way to handle a task (e.g., "all teams must use a Product Backlog"). In Disciplined Agile, you are provided with Process Goals and a range of strategies to achieve them:

- No "One Best Way": DA acknowledges that while a specific practice might be "best" for one team, it could be a disaster for another.
- The Toolkit Approach: Instead of a rigid script, DA provides a "menu" of techniques. For example, if your goal is to Coordinate Activities, your choices might range from a daily stand-up to an electronic kanban board or even a weekly status report, depending on your context.
- Informed Decisions: Choice is only "good" if you understand the consequences. The DA toolkit explains the trade-offs of each option so you can pick the one with the best "fit" for your current situation.

By embracing choice, teams avoid getting stuck in a "framework prison" and can evolve their Way of Working (WoW) as they learn more.
