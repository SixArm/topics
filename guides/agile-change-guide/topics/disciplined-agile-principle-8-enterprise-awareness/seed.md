# Disciplined Agile principle 8: Enterprise awareness

**Enterprise awareness: Look beyond your team’s immediate needs to consider what is best for the entire organization, such as following organizational roadmaps or leveraging existing assets.**

Enterprise awareness is the "big picture" principle. It reminds us that no team is an island; every team is part of a larger ecosystem.

While many agile frameworks focus only on what happens inside a single team, Disciplined Agile insists that teams have a responsibility to the entire organization. This means:

- Doing What’s Best for the Company: Sometimes, a solution that is "easiest" for your team might be a nightmare for the rest of the company. Enterprise awareness means choosing the path that provides the most value to the whole organization.
- Leveraging Existing Assets: Instead of building everything from scratch, "aware" teams look for existing code, tools, or data standards that they can reuse.
- Following Guidance: It means respecting organizational roadmaps, security protocols, and architectural standards so that the company doesn't end up with a chaotic mess of disconnected systems.
- Consistent Governance: Realizing that "governance" isn't a bad word. In DA, governance is seen as a way to provide a consistent, supportive environment where all teams can succeed together.

In short, Enterprise Awareness balances team autonomy with organizational alignment. It ensures that as you "Choose your WoW," you aren't accidentally breaking someone else's.
