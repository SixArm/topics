# Disciplined Agile principle 8: Enterprise awareness

**Enterprise awareness: Look beyond your team’s immediate needs to consider what is best for the entire organization, such as following organizational roadmaps or leveraging existing assets.**

Every team is part of a larger ecosystem, and decisions made in one team ripple
across the organization. Enterprise awareness demands that teams account for
those ripple effects.

In practice, this principle drives several behaviors. Teams choose solutions
that maximize value for the whole organization, even when a locally optimal
shortcut exists. They reuse existing code, tools, data standards, and
infrastructure rather than rebuilding from scratch. They respect organizational
roadmaps, security protocols, and architectural guidelines so the company avoids
a fragmented landscape of disconnected systems.

Governance plays a key role here. Disciplined Agile treats governance not as
bureaucratic overhead but as a mechanism for providing a consistent, supportive
environment where all teams can succeed together. Lightweight governance helps
maintain coherence across portfolios without stifling team autonomy.

The core tension this principle addresses is the balance between team autonomy
and organizational alignment. Disciplined Agile encourages teams to choose their
own way of working, but enterprise awareness ensures those choices do not
undermine other teams or conflict with strategic direction. Teams retain the
freedom to adapt their processes while staying connected to the bigger picture.
