# Mary Poppendieck quotations

Question: According to Mary Poppendieck, how should software development be viewed?

- [ ] As a customer service process focused on ticket resolution
- [ ] As a manufacturing process with standardized outputs
- [ ] As a design process that leads to more innovative solutions
- [ ] As a testing process where quality is verified at the end

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>As a design process that leads to more innovative solutions</p>
  <p>Mary Poppendieck argues that "software development is not a manufacturing process; it's a design process." This perspective is central to her philosophy because treating software development as a creative design discipline—rather than a repetitive manufacturing activity—leads to more innovative solutions and better outcomes for both developers and customers. This view aligns with lean principles and empowers teams to adapt and learn continuously.</p>
</details>
