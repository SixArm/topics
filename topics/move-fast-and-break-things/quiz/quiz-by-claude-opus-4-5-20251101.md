# Move fast and break things

Question: What is the primary criticism of the "move fast and break things" philosophy in technology development?

- [ ] It slows down the development process too much
- [ ] It can lead to unintended negative consequences for users and society
- [ ] It requires too much upfront planning and documentation
- [ ] It focuses too heavily on user safety testing

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>It can lead to unintended negative consequences for users and society</p>
  <p>Critics argue that the "move fast and break things" mentality can encourage reckless behavior and disregard for consequences, potentially harming users and society. This has led many companies to shift toward more deliberate approaches that balance innovation with responsible business practices, user safety, privacy, and long-term sustainability.</p>
</details>
