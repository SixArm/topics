# Military Strategy Books

Question: Which classic military strategy text emphasizes the principle of "knowing yourself, your enemy, and the terrain" as fundamental to strategic success?

- [ ] "On War" by Carl von Clausewitz
- [ ] "The Art of War" by Sun Tzu
- [ ] "The Mask of Command" by John Keegan
- [ ] "The 33 Strategies of War" by Robert Greene

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>"The Art of War" by Sun Tzu</p>
  <p>This ancient Chinese military treatise, written over 2,500 years ago, remains one of the most influential works on strategy ever written. Its core teaching—that victory comes from understanding yourself, your adversary, and the conditions in which you operate—has been widely adopted in business, technology, and leadership contexts. For technology professionals, this principle translates directly to understanding your team's capabilities, competitive landscape, and market conditions before making strategic decisions.</p>
</details>
