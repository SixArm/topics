# Disciplined Entrepreneurship by Bill Aulet

Question: According to Bill Aulet's framework, what is the core premise underlying the "Disciplined Entrepreneurship" approach?

- [ ] Successful entrepreneurs are born with innate talents that cannot be developed
- [ ] Entrepreneurship is primarily about taking risks and moving fast
- [ ] Entrepreneurship can be taught and requires a systematic, disciplined process
- [ ] Business success depends mainly on securing funding before developing a product

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Entrepreneurship can be taught and requires a systematic, disciplined process</p>
  <p>Aulet's framework is built on the premise that entrepreneurship is not an innate gift but rather a discipline that can be learned and practiced through a step-by-step approach. The book outlines six stages—Ideation, Market Segmentation, Product/Service Specifications, Business Model, Financials, and Pitching—providing entrepreneurs with structured tools and frameworks to systematically build successful ventures.</p>
</details>
