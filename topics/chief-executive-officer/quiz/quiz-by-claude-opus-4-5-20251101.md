# Chief Executive Officer (CEO)

Question: What is the primary reporting structure and accountability for a Chief Executive Officer?

- [ ] The CEO reports to the Chief Financial Officer and is accountable for budget management
- [ ] The CEO reports to the board of directors and is accountable for the company's performance and growth
- [ ] The CEO reports to shareholders directly and is accountable for daily operational tasks
- [ ] The CEO reports to the executive team and is accountable for technical implementations

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>The CEO reports to the board of directors and is accountable for the company's performance and growth</p>
  <p>The CEO is the highest-ranking executive officer in a company, and while they oversee operations and strategy, they report to the board of directors. Their accountability centers on the company's overall performance and growth, not just specific operational areas. This governance structure ensures proper oversight of executive leadership while allowing the CEO to make major corporate decisions and set the company's strategic vision.</p>
</details>
