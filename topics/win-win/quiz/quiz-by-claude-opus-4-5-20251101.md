# Win-win

Question: What does "win-win" mean in the context of business negotiations?

- [ ] A competitive strategy where the strongest party dominates the negotiation outcome
- [ ] A mutually beneficial outcome where all parties involved gain something valuable
- [ ] A zero-sum game where one party's gain equals another party's loss
- [ ] A compromise where all parties must sacrifice their primary goals

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>A mutually beneficial outcome where all parties involved gain something valuable</p>
  <p>Win-win describes a situation where all parties benefit from a decision or agreement. Unlike zero-sum scenarios where one party's gain is another's loss, win-win focuses on finding solutions that address each party's goals and needs, leading to a satisfactory outcome for everyone involved.</p>
</details>
