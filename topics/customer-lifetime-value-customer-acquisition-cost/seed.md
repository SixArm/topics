# Customer Lifetime Value : Customer Acquisition Cost (CLV:CAC)

The CLV:CAC ratio is the ultimate "efficiency metric" for a business. It measures the relationship between the total value a customer brings and the cost required to acquire them.
Think of it as the ROI of your growth engine: for every £1 you spend on marketing, how many pounds do you get back in profit over the long term?

## 1. The Formula

$$\text{CLV:CAC Ratio} = \frac{\text{Lifetime Value of a Customer}}{\text{Customer Acquisition Cost}}$$

- CLV (Lifetime Value): The total net profit a customer generates before they "churn" (stop buying).
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): The total sales and marketing spend divided by the number of new customers gained in that same period.

---

## 2. Benchmark "Health" Scores

Investors and consultants generally use the following rules of thumb to judge a business:

- 1:1 or lower (Failure): You are losing money on every customer. Scaling will only bankrupt you faster.
- 2:1 (Average): You are likely covering costs but don't have much room for error or significant profit after overhead.
- 3:1 (The "Golden Ratio"): This is the industry standard for a healthy, high-growth company (especially in SaaS). It means you have a sustainable engine.
- 5:1+ (Under-invested?): While great for profit, a very high ratio might mean you are being too cautious and should spend more on marketing to grow faster and capture market share.

---

## 3. The "Missing Link": Payback Period

CLV:CAC tells you how much you make, but not how fast.

- A company could have a great 5:1 ratio, but if it takes 4 years for the customer to pay back their CAC, the company might run out of cash before it sees that profit.
- Target: Most healthy startups aim for a Payback Period of <12 months.

## 4. How to Improve the Ratio

To make this number go up, you have two logical levers (a mini-driver tree):

- Raise the CLV: Increase prices, upsell existing customers, or improve the product so customers stay longer (reduce churn).
- Lower the CAC: Improve marketing conversion rates, find cheaper lead sources, or use "virality" and word-of-mouth.
