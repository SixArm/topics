# All-hands meeting

Question: What is a primary purpose of an all-hands meeting in an organization?

- [ ] To conduct individual performance reviews with each employee
- [ ] To replace daily standup meetings for development teams
- [ ] To foster transparency, alignment, and unity across the entire company
- [ ] To negotiate salary adjustments with department managers

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To foster transparency, alignment, and unity across the entire company</p>
  <p>All-hands meetings bring together all employees from various levels and departments to communicate important information, provide updates, and promote alignment. They help employees understand how their individual roles contribute to the overall success of the company, shape company culture, and provide opportunities for dialogue between leadership and employees through Q&A sessions.</p>
</details>
