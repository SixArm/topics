# Perfect is the enemy of good

Question: What is the primary risk of perfectionism in technology projects according to the aphorism "perfect is the enemy of good"?

- [ ] It ensures higher quality deliverables by catching all defects
- [ ] It consumes excessive time and resources while yielding diminishing returns and preventing progress
- [ ] It motivates team members to produce their best work
- [ ] It establishes industry-standard benchmarks for product quality

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>It consumes excessive time and resources while yielding diminishing returns and preventing progress</p>
  <p>The aphorism warns that pursuing perfection can consume valuable time, resources, and energy, often yielding diminishing returns. By fixating on minute details or endlessly seeking improvements, technology professionals may miss opportunities for growth, learning, or completing important tasks. A pragmatic approach that embraces "good enough" leads to greater productivity and the ability to adapt and evolve.</p>
</details>
