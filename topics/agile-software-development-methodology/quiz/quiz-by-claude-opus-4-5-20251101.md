# Agile software development methodology

Question: What is the primary approach that distinguishes Agile software development from traditional waterfall methodologies?

- [ ] Delivering a complete product only at the end of a lengthy development cycle
- [ ] Following a rigid, sequential development process with fixed requirements
- [ ] Iterative and incremental development with continuous delivery of working software
- [ ] Limiting customer involvement until final product delivery

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Iterative and incremental development with continuous delivery of working software</p>
  <p>Agile software development is fundamentally an iterative and incremental approach that prioritizes continuous delivery of working software in small increments. This contrasts with traditional waterfall methodologies that deliver a complete product only at the end of the development cycle. Agile embraces flexibility, customer collaboration throughout the process, and the ability to adapt to changing requirementsâ€”making continuous delivery of small, working increments a core distinguishing characteristic.</p>
</details>
