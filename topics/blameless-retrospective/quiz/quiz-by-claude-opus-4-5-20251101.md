# Blameless retrospective

Question: What is the primary purpose of a blameless retrospective in agile software development?

- [ ] To document individual performance metrics for annual reviews
- [ ] To identify issues and find ways to improve the process without placing blame on individuals
- [ ] To assign responsibility for project failures to specific team members
- [ ] To create a formal record of mistakes for management reporting

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To identify issues and find ways to improve the process without placing blame on individuals</p>
  <p>A blameless retrospective focuses on identifying problems and areas for improvement in an open, non-judgmental environment. By removing blame from the discussion, team members feel comfortable sharing their experiences and observations, which promotes a culture of continuous improvement and enables teams to work together more effectively.</p>
</details>
