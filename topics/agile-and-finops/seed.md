# Agile and finops

In the modern enterprise, FinOps (Cloud Financial Operations) is considered the "third leg" of the stool alongside Agile and DevOps. While Agile focuses on speed of development and DevOps focuses on speed of delivery, FinOps ensures that this speed doesn't lead to out-of-control cloud costs. 

The relationship between Agile and FinOps is built on decentralised accountability. In Agile environments, developers have the power to spin up cloud resources instantly; FinOps provides the framework to ensure they do so responsibly. 

Iterative Optimization: FinOps follows a cyclical lifecycle—Inform, Optimize, and Operate—that mirrors Agile’s "Plan, Do, Check, Act" loops. 

Cost as a First-Class Metric: Just as "Definition of Done" in Agile might include testing, "Agile FinOps" integrates cost-efficiency into the sprint backlog. 

Cross-Functional Collaboration: FinOps breaks down silos between Finance, Engineering, and Product teams, much like how Agile unites developers and business stakeholders. 

## Key Benefits of Combining Them

Balanced Velocity: Ensures that rapid feature releases are financially sustainable and don't overwhelm the budget. 

Data-Driven Decisions: Teams use real-time cloud spend data to prioritise the product roadmap based on ROI rather than just functionality. 

Shared Responsibility: Moves cost management away from a central finance "police" to the engineering teams who actually trigger the spend.
