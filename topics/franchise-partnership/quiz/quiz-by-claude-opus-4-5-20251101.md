# Franchise partnership

Question: What is the primary relationship structure in a franchise partnership?

- [ ] The franchisee develops the business model and licenses it to multiple franchisors
- [ ] Two independent businesses merge their operations under a new shared brand
- [ ] The franchisor grants rights to operate a business using its established brand, business model, and intellectual property to the franchisee
- [ ] The franchisee and franchisor share equal ownership of all business assets and profits

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>The franchisor grants rights to operate a business using its established brand, business model, and intellectual property to the franchisee</p>
  <p>A franchise partnership is fundamentally structured around the franchisor (who owns the established business concept, brand, and intellectual property) granting operational rights to a franchisee. The franchisee pays fees and royalties in exchange for using the franchisor's brand, proven business model, training, and ongoing support, while adhering to established standards and guidelines.</p>
</details>
