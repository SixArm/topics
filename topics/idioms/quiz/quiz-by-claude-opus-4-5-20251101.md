# Idioms

Question: What is the key characteristic that distinguishes idioms from literal language?

- [ ] Idioms always contain exactly three words
- [ ] Idioms are only used in written communication
- [ ] Idioms have meanings different from the literal meanings of their individual words
- [ ] Idioms can be understood by translating each word separately

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Idioms have meanings different from the literal meanings of their individual words</p>
  <p>Idioms are phrases or expressions whose meaning cannot be understood by simply translating the individual words. For example, "barking up the wrong tree" means pursuing a mistaken course of actionâ€”a meaning that cannot be derived from the literal words "barking" and "tree." This is why idioms can be challenging for non-native speakers who may try to interpret them word by word rather than understanding them through usage and context.</p>
