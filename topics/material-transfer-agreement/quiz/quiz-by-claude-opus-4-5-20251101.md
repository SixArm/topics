# Material Transfer Agreement (MTA)

Question: What is the primary purpose of a Material Transfer Agreement (MTA)?

- [ ] To permanently transfer ownership of research materials to the recipient organization
- [ ] To establish a joint venture between two research institutions
- [ ] To govern the transfer of tangible research materials while the provider retains ownership and control
- [ ] To license software and digital assets between technology companies

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To govern the transfer of tangible research materials while the provider retains ownership and control</p>
  <p>An MTA is a legal contract used when a researcher or organization wants to obtain materials from another organization for research purposes, but the provider wishes to retain ownership and control over the materials. MTAs facilitate scientific research by enabling sharing while protecting intellectual property rights and addressing liabilities.</p>
</details>
