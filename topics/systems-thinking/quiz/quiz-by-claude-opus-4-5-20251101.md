# Systems thinking

Question: What is the primary focus of systems thinking when applied to software development?

- [ ] Optimizing individual features and sprints in isolation for maximum efficiency
- [ ] Viewing interconnected relationships and understanding how changes affect the entire ecosystem
- [ ] Prioritizing technical tools and processes over human factors
- [ ] Minimizing feedback loops to reduce complexity in decision-making

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Viewing interconnected relationships and understanding how changes affect the entire ecosystem</p>
  <p>Systems thinking is a holistic perspective that views interconnected relationships rather than isolated components. This approach helps identify bottlenecks, dependencies, and emergent properties that arise from the interaction of system components, including both technical elements and human factors like team dynamics and organizational culture.</p>
