## Business capabilities

Henderson also provides resources aimed at managers and business leaders. These cover strategic planning, performance measurement, and operational management practices.

- **Objectives and Key Results (OKRs):** Goal-setting frameworks that align teams around measurable outcomes.
- **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):** Metrics for tracking progress toward strategic objectives.
- **Strategic Balanced Scorecards (SBS):** Multi-perspective evaluation tools that connect financial, customer, process, and learning dimensions.
- **Value Stream Mappings (VSMs):** Visual tools for analyzing and optimizing the flow of work through an organization.
- **Statements of Work (SOWs):** Templates for defining project scope, deliverables, and expectations.

The common thread is that each of these practices benefits from standardized templates and documented best practices, which is exactly what Henderson's repositories provide. For technology professionals who are moving into leadership roles, these resources are particularly valuable because they bridge the vocabulary and methodology gaps between engineering and management.

For technology professionals evaluating guides, frameworks, or repositories, understanding the author's background serves several practical purposes:

- **Credibility assessment:** Knowing that content comes from someone with hands-on experience across startups, enterprises, and nonprofits increases confidence in its practical applicability.
- **Bias awareness:** Every author brings a perspective shaped by their experience. Henderson's dual technology-and-business focus means his materials tend to emphasize the connection between engineering decisions and business outcomes.
- **Context matching:** A reader working at a Fortune 500 company can trust that the author has operated in that environment, just as a startup founder can.
