# Work-for-hire agreement

Question: What is the defining characteristic of a work-for-hire agreement regarding intellectual property?

- [ ] The creator retains full ownership of the work and licenses it to the hiring party
- [ ] Ownership is split equally between the hiring party and the creator
- [ ] The hiring party owns the work produced, rather than the individual who created it
- [ ] The creator owns the work until full payment is received, then ownership transfers

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>The hiring party owns the work produced, rather than the individual who created it</p>
  <p>A work-for-hire agreement specifies that the work produced is owned by the hiring party, not the freelancer or independent contractor who created it.
