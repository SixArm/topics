# The Architecture Inception Canvas (AIC) is a lightweight, one-page tool designed to kickstart software projects by capturing essential architectural drivers and goals. Based on the Business Model Canvas concept, it focuses on "greenfield" projects (new development) to ensure the entire product team understands the "why" and "what" before diving into deep technical design.

The AIC structures early project conversations into collaborative sections that map directly to the arc42 documentation template. These sections include the business case (economic drivers), quality goals (the top three to five quality attributes such as scalability or security), a functional overview of critical high-level requirements, and a business context that shows the system as a black box with its external users and neighboring systems. The canvas also captures organizational and technical constraints, risks and challenges, and architectural hypotheses representing initial design ideas and significant early decisions.

The canvas solves a common problem: misalignment between stakeholders and developers at the start of a project. Rather than lengthy workshops or sprawling documents, the AIC compresses essential architectural thinking into a single visual framework that the whole team can review and debate. Fields for iteration number and date make it a living artifact that teams update as understanding deepens.

Once the canvas stabilizes, its contents feed directly into a full arc42 architecture document, giving teams a smooth transition from initial exploration to structured documentation. This makes the AIC particularly valuable for organizations already using or adopting the arc42 template.

Official templates are available in PDF, Miro, and PowerPoint formats from the arc42 site. Teams typically complete an initial version in a single collaborative session, then refine it over subsequent iterations as requirements solidify and technical decisions become clearer.
