# Teamwork books

Question: Which book specifically addresses the common pitfalls that prevent teams from functioning effectively, presenting them as a framework of interconnected dysfunctions?

- [ ] "Team of Teams: New Rules of Engagement for a Complex World" by General Stanley McChrystal
- [ ] "The Culture Code: The Secrets of Highly Successful Groups" by Daniel Coyle
- [ ] "The Five Dysfunctions of a Team" by Patrick Lencioni
- [ ] "Drive: The Surprising Truth About What Motivates Us" by Daniel H. Pink

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>"The Five Dysfunctions of a Team" by Patrick Lencioni</p>
  <p>This book explores the common pitfalls and challenges that teams face through a framework of five interconnected dysfunctions: absence of trust, fear of conflict, lack of commitment, avoidance of accountability, and inattention to results. It offers practical strategies for building stronger, more effective teams by addressing each dysfunction systematically.</p>
</details>
