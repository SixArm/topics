# Over the horizon

Question: What does "over the horizon" thinking primarily emphasize in strategic planning?

- [ ] Focusing exclusively on immediate operational concerns
- [ ] Anticipating long-term trends, technological advancements, and potential disruptions
- [ ] Limiting planning to visible and currently known factors
- [ ] Avoiding consideration of future risks to reduce complexity

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Anticipating long-term trends, technological advancements, and potential disruptions</p>
  <p>"Over the horizon" thinking in strategic planning involves considering possibilities beyond the immediate present, including long-term trends, emerging technologies, and potential disruptions that may affect an organization's future. This forward-looking perspective enables proactive decision-making to stay ahead of changes rather than merely reacting to them.</p>
</details>
