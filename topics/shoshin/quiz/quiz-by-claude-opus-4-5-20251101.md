# Shoshin

Question: What does the Japanese term "shoshin" mean, and how should technology professionals apply it?

- [ ] "Expert's certainty" — relying on proven expertise to make quick decisions without reconsidering alternatives
- [ ] "Beginner's mind" — approaching situations with openness, curiosity, and humility regardless of your experience level
- [ ] "Master's discipline" — strictly following established best practices and methodologies without deviation
- [ ] "Senior's authority" — using seniority and experience to guide teams toward predetermined solutions

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>"Beginner's mind" — approaching situations with openness, curiosity, and humility regardless of your experience level</p>
  <p>Shoshin encourages technology professionals to maintain an open, curious mindset free from preconceptions, even when working in areas where they have significant expertise. This approach fosters continuous learning, receptiveness to new ideas, fresh perspectives on familiar problems, and the humility to recognize there is always more to learn—essential qualities in rapidly evolving technology fields.</p>
</details>
