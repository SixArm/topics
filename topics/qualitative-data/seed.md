# Qualitative data

Qualitative data is non-numerical, descriptive information that captures concepts, opinions, emotions, and lived experiences to explain the "why" and "how" behind behaviors. Gathered through interviews, focus groups, open-ended surveys, and observations, this data type offers deep insights and context. Unlike quantitative data, it is subjective and analyzed by identifying themes and patterns. 

Key Aspects of Qualitative Data:

- Definition: Information not represented by numbers, focusing on meaning and quality.

- Data Sources: Interview transcripts, focus group notes, open-ended survey responses, audio/visual recordings, and personal narratives.

- Key Characteristics: Subjective, individualized, descriptive, and context-driven.

- Analysis Methods: Involves coding (labeling text), thematic analysis, and interpreting data to find patterns.

- Methods of Collection: In-depth interviews, focus groups, observations, and case studies. 

Examples of Qualitative Data:

- Interview Transcript: "The product felt flimsy, and I was worried it would break."

- Open-ended Survey Response: "I prefer this app because it is intuitive, but I dislike the color scheme."

- Observation Notes: "Participants in the focus group showed frustration when discussing the checkout process". 

Qualitative data is often analyzed using software like NVivo, Dedoose, or Taguette to manage and organize the text or media.
