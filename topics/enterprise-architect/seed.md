# Enterprise architect

An Enterprise Architect (EA) is a senior IT professional who aligns an organization's technology infrastructure, data, and applications with its overall business strategy. They analyze, design, and plan IT, software, and hardware improvements to drive digital transformation and efficiency. Key skills include strategic planning, cloud technology, data modeling, and stakeholder communication. 

Key Responsibilities and Skills:

- Strategic Alignment: Bridging the gap between business goals and technical solutions to create, maintain, and implement enterprise-wide IT blueprints.

- System Oversight: Managing, upgrading, and securing an organization's technology stack.

- Governance: Establishing standards, architectural policies, and review boards to ensure compliance and system interoperability.

- Change Management: Developing strategies to guide the organization through technological and operational shifts.

- Tools: Using modeling tools like Sparx Systems Enterprise Architect to visualize and manage complex systems. 

Career Information: 

- Background: Typically requires a bachelor's degree in computer science or IT, with 10+ years of experience in related IT roles.

- Pathways: Often progresses from roles like software engineer or systems analyst to EA, with potential for roles such as Chief Technology Officer (CTO) or Chief Information Officer (CIO).
