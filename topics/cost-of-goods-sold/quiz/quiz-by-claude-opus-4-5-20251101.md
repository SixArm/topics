# Cost of goods sold (COGS)

Question: What does Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) represent in a company's financial statements?

- [ ] All operating expenses including rent, utilities, and administrative salaries
- [ ] The total revenue generated from product sales during a period
- [ ] The direct costs associated with producing or acquiring the goods a company sells
- [ ] The market value of all inventory held at the end of a fiscal year

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>The direct costs associated with producing or acquiring the goods a company sells</p>
  <p>COGS specifically includes direct costs such as materials, labor, and overhead expenses directly tied to production. Indirect costs like rent, electricity, and salaries of employees not directly involved in production are classified as operating expenses, not COGS. Understanding this distinction is crucial for technology professionals analyzing profitability, pricing strategies, and financial health of companiesâ€”particularly when evaluating software or hardware product margins.</p>
</details>
