# Usability

Question: According to usability principles, what are the five key aspects that determine how effectively users can interact with a product or system?

- [ ] Speed, Scalability, Security, Stability, and Support
- [ ] Learnability, Efficiency, Memorability, Error Prevention, and User Satisfaction
- [ ] Accessibility, Responsiveness, Compatibility, Performance, and Reliability
- [ ] Design, Development, Deployment, Documentation, and Debugging

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Learnability, Efficiency, Memorability, Error Prevention, and User Satisfaction</p>
  <p>These five key aspects form the foundation of usability evaluation. Learnability ensures users can quickly understand a system; Efficiency minimizes steps to complete tasks; Memorability helps users retain knowledge after periods of inactivity; Error Prevention enables users to discover and correct mistakes easily; and User Satisfaction ensures users feel positive about their experience. Together, these aspects directly impact user experience and determine whether a product achieves its intended purpose effectively.</p>
</details>
