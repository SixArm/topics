# Equal opportunity

Question: What is the primary purpose of equal opportunity laws and regulations in the workplace?

- [ ] To ensure companies hire a specific quota of candidates from underrepresented groups
- [ ] To guarantee equal salaries for all employees regardless of their role or experience
- [ ] To protect individuals from unfair treatment and provide mechanisms for addressing discrimination complaints
- [ ] To require mandatory diversity training for all managers

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To protect individuals from unfair treatment and provide mechanisms for addressing discrimination complaints</p>
  <p>Equal opportunity laws and regulations are designed to prohibit discrimination and promote equality by protecting individuals from unfair treatment based on factors such as race, gender, age, ethnicity, religion, disability, or socioeconomic status. These laws provide legal mechanisms for addressing and resolving complaints of discrimination, ensuring everyone has an equal chance to compete for opportunities, be treated fairly, and access the same benefits and career advancement opportunities.</p>
</details>
