# On the radar

Question: In a business context, what does the phrase "on the radar" typically mean?

- [ ] A technology that uses radio waves to detect physical objects
- [ ] Something that has been completed and archived
- [ ] Something being monitored or considered for future reference or action
- [ ] A project that has been officially canceled

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Something being monitored or considered for future reference or action</p>
  <p>The idiom "on the radar" comes from radar systems that detect and track objects over time. In business settings, it refers to monitoring potential opportunities, risks, competitors, or partners that may warrant future attention or action.</p>
</details>
