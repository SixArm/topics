# Disciplined Agile (DA)

Disciplined Agile (DA) is a comprehensive process-decision toolkit designed to help individuals, teams, and enterprises optimize their Way of Working (WoW). Unlike rigid frameworks like Scrum or SAFe, DA is a hybrid approach that integrates hundreds of practices from agile, lean, and even traditional (waterfall) methodologies, providing a context-sensitive foundation for business agility.

## Core Philosophy: Choose Your WoW

The central idea of DA is that "context counts". Because every team and organization is unique, DA avoids a one-size-fits-all approach. Instead, it provides:

- A Hybrid Toolkit: It pulls strategies from Scrum, Kanban, Extreme Programming (XP), Lean Startup, and the PMBOK® Guide.
- Goal-Driven Guidance: It uses "Goal Diagrams" to help teams explore options and understand the trade-offs of different decisions.
- Freedom and Flexibility: DA encourages teams to be "pragmatic" by choosing the practices that work best for their specific situation rather than strictly following a single framework.

## The Four Layers of the DA Toolkit

DA is structured into four layers to address different levels of organizational agility:

- Foundation: Covers the DA mindset, principles, roles, and basic concepts of agile and lean.
- Disciplined DevOps: Focuses on streamlining IT solution development and operations, including security and data management.
- Value Streams: Helps visualize and improve the end-to-end flow of value to customers.
- Disciplined Agile Enterprise (DAE): Addresses the broader enterprise activities like finance, legal, and vendor management to foster overall organizational responsiveness.

## Key Lifecycle Options

DA recognizes that different projects require different approaches and supports several lifecycles:

- Agile: An iteration-based lifecycle based on Scrum.
- Lean: A continuous flow-based lifecycle based on Kanban.
- Continuous Delivery: Agile or Lean versions for frequent releasing.
- Exploratory: Based on Lean Startup for high-uncertainty research projects.
- Program: Designed for a "team of teams" in large initiatives.
