# Disciplined Agile (DA)

Disciplined Agile (DA) is a process-decision toolkit that helps individuals, teams, and enterprises optimize their way of working. Unlike prescriptive frameworks such as Scrum or SAFe, DA is a hybrid approach that integrates practices from agile, lean, and traditional methodologies, providing a context-sensitive foundation for business agility.

The central idea of DA is that context counts. Every team and organization is unique, so DA avoids a one-size-fits-all prescription. It pulls strategies from Scrum, Kanban, Extreme Programming, Lean Startup, and the PMBOK Guide. It uses goal diagrams to help teams explore options and understand the trade-offs of different decisions. It encourages teams to choose the practices that work best for their specific situation rather than strictly following a single framework.

DA is structured into four layers addressing different levels of organizational agility. The foundation layer covers the DA mindset, principles, roles, and core concepts. Disciplined DevOps focuses on streamlining solution development and operations, including security and data management. Value streams help visualize and improve end-to-end flow of value to customers. Disciplined Agile Enterprise addresses broader activities like finance, legal, and vendor management to foster organizational responsiveness.

DA recognizes that different projects require different approaches and supports several lifecycles. The agile lifecycle is iteration-based, drawing from Scrum. The lean lifecycle uses continuous flow based on Kanban. Continuous delivery variants enable frequent releasing. The exploratory lifecycle applies Lean Startup thinking for high-uncertainty work. The program lifecycle coordinates large initiatives across multiple teams.

DA gives technology professionals a structured way to reason about process choices without locking them into a rigid methodology.
