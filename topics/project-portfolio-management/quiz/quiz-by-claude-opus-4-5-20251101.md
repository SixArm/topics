# Project Portfolio Management (PPM)

Question: What is the primary purpose of Project Portfolio Management (PPM)?

- [ ] To track individual developer productivity metrics across all projects
- [ ] To replace project managers with automated scheduling tools
- [ ] To centrally manage and align projects with organizational business objectives while optimizing resource allocation and maximizing business value
- [ ] To ensure all projects use the same technology stack

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To centrally manage and align projects with organizational business objectives while optimizing resource allocation and maximizing business value</p>
  <p>PPM is a strategic approach that involves centralized oversight of an organization's project portfolio. Its core purpose is to ensure projects are prioritized based on strategic alignment, resources are allocated optimally across initiatives, risks are managed at the portfolio level, and the overall portfolio delivers maximum business value aligned with organizational goals.</p>
</details>
