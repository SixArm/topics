# Business strategy and business tactics

Question: What is the key difference between business strategy and business tactics?

- [ ] Business tactics are formulated by executives while business strategy is handled by operational staff
- [ ] Business strategy focuses on long-term goals and overall direction, while business tactics are specific short-term actions to execute that strategy
- [ ] Business strategy deals with day-to-day operations, while business tactics address the company's mission and vision
- [ ] Business tactics require more resources than business strategy because they involve higher-level planning

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Business strategy focuses on long-term goals and overall direction, while business tactics are specific short-term actions to execute that strategy</p>
  <p>Business strategy addresses fundamental questions about the organization's mission, vision, competitive positioning, and value creation at the enterprise level. Business tactics are the operational, detailed actions and initiatives employed to execute that strategy—such as marketing campaigns, pricing decisions, and resource allocation—that respond to immediate circumstances and support day-to-day activities within the strategic framework.</p>
</details>
