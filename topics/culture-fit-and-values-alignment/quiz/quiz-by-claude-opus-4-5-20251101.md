# Culture fit and values alignment

Question: What is the key difference between culture fit and values alignment when evaluating a candidate for a technology team?

- [ ] Culture fit focuses on personal beliefs and ethics, while values alignment focuses on work style and attitudes
- [ ] Culture fit relates to work style and organizational norms, while values alignment relates to personal beliefs and organizational principles
- [ ] They are interchangeable concepts that both measure how well someone will perform technically
- [ ] Culture fit measures technical skills, while values alignment measures soft skills

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Culture fit relates to work style and organizational norms, while values alignment relates to personal beliefs and organizational principles</p>
  <p>Culture fit refers to how well an individual's work style, communication approach, and attitudes align with the norms of an organization (such as pace, decision-making processes, and social dynamics). Values alignment refers to how well an individual's personal values and beliefs align with the organization's principles (such as integrity, social responsibility, and ethical practices).
