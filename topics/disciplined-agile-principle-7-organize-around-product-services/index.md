# Disciplined Agile principle 7: Organize around products/services

Disciplined Agile's seventh principle calls for structuring teams and work around value streams rather than traditional functional silos. Instead of shuffling tasks between departments, organizations should build stable, long-lived teams aligned directly to the products and services that deliver value to customers.

Traditional project-based thinking treats work as temporary. A project starts, consumes its budget, and ends, often disbanding the team that built the expertise. Product-oriented thinking reverses this. Teams stay together, accumulate deep domain knowledge, and take ongoing responsibility for the quality and evolution of what they deliver. This continuity leads to better architectural decisions, faster onboarding, and stronger ownership.

The principle demands cross-functional teams. Rather than maintaining separate testing, development, and design departments, each product team includes everyone needed to deliver value: developers, testers, designers, business analysts, and domain experts. This eliminates the handoffs and wait times that accumulate when work moves from silo to silo. A feature that once required coordination across four departments can move from concept to delivery within a single team.

Organizations applying this principle identify their value streams, the end-to-end sequences of activities that produce outcomes customers care about, and align people directly to those streams. This alignment ensures that team goals and organizational goals point in the same direction.

The practical result is reduced complexity. Teams spend less time coordinating across organizational boundaries and more time building. Feedback loops shorten because the people who design, build, test, and support a product sit together and share context continuously. Accountability becomes clear: one team owns one product, and success or failure is unambiguous.

Organizing around products and services turns the organizational structure itself into an enabler of agility rather than an obstacle to it.
