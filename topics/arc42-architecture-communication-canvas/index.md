# Arc42 Architecture Communication Canvas (ACC)

The Arc42 Architecture Communication Canvas (ACC) is a structured one-page tool for documenting and communicating the essential aspects of a software architecture. Developed as part of the arc42 ecosystem, it gives teams a shared format for capturing what matters most about a system without requiring lengthy documentation.

The canvas organizes architecture knowledge into nine interconnected sections. Value Proposition defines the system's core business case and economic drivers. Key Stakeholder identifies who receives value, who pays, and who contributes. Core Functions captures the most important features, use cases, and supported processes. Quality Requirements addresses non-functional goals such as speed, scalability, reliability, usability, and security.

Business Context maps external interfaces, neighbouring systems, and important actors. Components and Modules describes the major building blocks, including subsystems, packages, and services. Technologies records the programming languages, frameworks, databases, infrastructure, and operational tooling in use. Core Decisions documents the significant architectural choices, both successful and problematic, that shaped the system's current state.

The final section, Risks and Missing Information, is deliberately provocative. It asks teams to surface known problems, identify gaps in understanding, and name what hinders them from delivering value faster. This section often reveals the most actionable insights.

The ACC works best as a collaborative workshop artifact. Architects, developers, and stakeholders fill it out together, forcing alignment on priorities and exposing conflicting assumptions early. It serves as both a discovery tool for existing systems and a planning tool for new ones. Because it fits on a single page, it remains accessible to non-technical stakeholders while still capturing enough detail to guide technical conversations.
