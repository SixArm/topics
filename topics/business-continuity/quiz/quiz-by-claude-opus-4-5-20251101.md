# Business continuity

Question: What is the primary purpose of conducting a Business Impact Analysis (BIA) as part of business continuity planning?

- [ ] To create detailed disaster recovery procedures for IT systems
- [ ] To identify critical business functions and prioritize their recovery
- [ ] To train employees on emergency evacuation routes
- [ ] To calculate the total cost of insurance coverage needed

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To identify critical business functions and prioritize their recovery</p>
  <p>A Business Impact Analysis (BIA) is the process of identifying critical business functions and understanding the impact of their disruption on the organization. This analysis helps prioritize which functions and processes should be recovered first, ensuring that the most essential operations are restored quickly to minimize overall business impact during a disruption.</p>
</details>
