# Office of Population Censuses and Surveys Classification of Interventions and Procedures version 4) is the clinical coding standard used in the United Kingdom to record surgical operations, procedures, and medical interventions. [1, 2] 

Office of Population Censuses and Surveys (OPCS) Classification of Interventions and Procedures version 4 is abbreviated OPCS-4.

Maintained by [NHS England](https://digital.nhs.uk/services/terminology-and-classifications) on behalf of the UK, it translates complex medical descriptions into a standardised alphanumeric format to support statistical analysis, service planning, and financial reimbursement. [3, 4] 

## Core Structure and Codes

* Alphanumeric Format: Codes are typically four characters long, starting with a letter that denotes a specific chapter, followed by three numbers (e.g., M72.1 for Partial urethrectomy). [5, 6] 
* Anatomical Chapters: The classification is split into 24 chapters mostly organised by body system (e.g., Chapter K for Heart, Chapter S for Skin). [1, 2] 
* Volumes: It traditionally consists of two main volumes: a Tabular List (codes and descriptions) and an Alphabetical Index for lookups. [1, 2] 
* Subsidiary Codes: Special chapters (Y and Z) provide additional detail on the method of operation or the specific anatomical site and laterality. [5, 7] 

## Current Version and Updates

* Current Version: The version mandated for use since 1 April 2023 is OPCS-4.10.
* Upcoming Release: A new version, OPCS-4.11, is scheduled for implementation on 1 April 2026.
* Digital Migration: Starting with version 4.11, the classification is moving from traditional paper volumes to a purely online [NHS Classifications Browser](https://classbrowser.nhs.uk/). [8, 9, 10, 11, 12] 

## Role in the NHS Ecosystem

* Reimbursement: Along with ICD-10 (which codes diagnoses), OPCS-4 is used to calculate [National Tariff payments](https://digital.nhs.uk/developer/guides-and-documentation/building-healthcare-software/clinical-coding-classifications-and-terminology) to hospitals for the care they provide. [3, 6] 
* Complementary Standards:
* ICD-10: Used for coding what is wrong (diagnoses).
* SNOMED CT: Used for direct patient care and detailed clinical records, whereas OPCS-4 is primarily for secondary uses like statistics and billing. [4, 6, 13, 14]
