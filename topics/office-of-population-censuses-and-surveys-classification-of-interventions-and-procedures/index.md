# Office of Population Censuses and Surveys Classification of Interventions and Procedures (OPCS-4)

The Office of Population Censuses and Surveys Classification of Interventions and Procedures version 4 (OPCS-4) is the clinical coding standard used in the United Kingdom to record surgical operations, procedures, and medical interventions.

OPCS-4 is maintained by NHS England on behalf of the UK. It translates complex medical descriptions into a standardized alphanumeric format to support statistical analysis, service planning, and financial reimbursement. Codes are typically four characters long, starting with a letter that denotes a specific chapter, followed by three numbers. For example, M72.1 represents Partial urethrectomy. The classification is split into 24 chapters organized mostly by body system, such as Chapter K for Heart and Chapter S for Skin.

The classification traditionally consists of two main volumes: a Tabular List containing codes and descriptions, and an Alphabetical Index for lookups. Special chapters Y and Z provide additional detail on the method of operation or the specific anatomical site and laterality.

The version mandated for use since 1 April 2023 is OPCS-4.10, with OPCS-4.11 scheduled for implementation on 1 April 2026. Starting with version 4.11, the classification is moving from traditional paper volumes to a purely online NHS Classifications Browser.

OPCS-4 plays a central role in NHS financial operations. Alongside ICD-10, which codes diagnoses, OPCS-4 is used to calculate National Tariff payments to hospitals for the care they provide. While SNOMED CT serves direct patient care and detailed clinical records, OPCS-4 is designed primarily for secondary uses such as statistics, service planning, and billing. This distinction makes OPCS-4 the backbone of procedural data analysis across the UK health system.
