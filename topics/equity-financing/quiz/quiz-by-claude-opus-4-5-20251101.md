# Equity financing

Question: What is the primary trade-off a company makes when using equity financing to raise capital?

- [ ] The company must pay fixed interest rates regardless of profitability
- [ ] The company gives up partial ownership and control in exchange for capital that does not require repayment
- [ ] The company is required to repay the principal amount within a fixed timeframe
- [ ] The company must maintain a minimum credit rating to satisfy investors

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>The company gives up partial ownership and control in exchange for capital that does not require repayment</p>
  <p>Equity financing involves selling ownership shares (equity) to investors who become part-owners of the company. Unlike debt financing, the capital raised does not need to be repaidâ€”instead, investors receive returns through dividends or capital gains. However, this dilutes the founders' ownership and control, as new shareholders gain voting rights and may have different opinions and goals for the company's direction.</p>
