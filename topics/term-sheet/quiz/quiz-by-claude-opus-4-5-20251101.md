# Term sheet

Question: What is the primary purpose of a term sheet in an investment deal?

- [ ] To serve as the final legally binding contract between investor and company
- [ ] To outline the terms and conditions as a non-binding basis for negotiations
- [ ] To provide audited financial statements to potential investors
- [ ] To register the company's securities with regulatory authorities

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To outline the terms and conditions as a non-binding basis for negotiations</p>
  <p>A term sheet is a non-binding document that outlines the basic terms of a potential investment, including valuation, investment amount, and investor rights. It serves as the foundation for negotiations and, once agreed upon, forms the basis for drafting the detailed, legally binding investment agreement.</p>
</details>
