# Initial Public Offering (IPO)

Question: What is the primary role of investment banks in an Initial Public Offering (IPO)?

- [ ] To provide long-term loans to the company going public
- [ ] To acquire a controlling stake in the company
- [ ] To underwrite the offering, help determine share pricing, and sell shares to investors
- [ ] To manage the company's day-to-day operations after going public

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To underwrite the offering, help determine share pricing, and sell shares to investors</p>
  <p>Investment banks serve as underwriters in the IPO process. They help the company determine the appropriate price for shares, sell them to institutional and retail investors, and assist with necessary regulatory filings including the prospectus that details the company's financial and business operations.</p>
</details>
