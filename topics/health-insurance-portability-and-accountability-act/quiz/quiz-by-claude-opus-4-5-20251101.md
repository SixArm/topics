# Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)

Question: What does the HIPAA Security Rule specifically require covered entities to implement for electronic protected health information (ePHI)?

- [ ] Annual security audits conducted by external government agencies
- [ ] Administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability
- [ ] Mandatory encryption using only government-approved algorithms
- [ ] Public disclosure of all data handling procedures on company websites

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability</p>
  <p>The HIPAA Security Rule establishes standards specifically for electronic PHI (ePHI) and requires covered entities to implement three categories of safeguards: administrative (policies and procedures), physical (facility access controls), and technical (access controls, encryption, audit controls). These safeguards work together to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHIâ€”the core security triad that technology professionals must address when building or maintaining systems that handle health information.</p>
