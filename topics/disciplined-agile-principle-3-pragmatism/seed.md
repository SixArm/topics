# Disciplined Agile principle 3: Pragmatism

"Pragmatism: Focus on being effective rather than dogmatically 'being agile'. This may involve using lean or even traditional strategies if they fit the situation better."

Principle 3: Pragmatism is the "reality check" of the Disciplined Agile mindset. It moves away from agile dogmatism—the idea that you must follow specific rules to be "true agile"—and focuses instead on being effective.

The core tenets of this principle include:

- Doing What Works: If a traditional (waterfall) technique or a specific lean practice is the most effective solution for your current problem, use it. The goal is to succeed, not to be a "Scrum purist."
- Context Over Rules: Every team faces different constraints, skill levels, and regulatory environments. Pragmatism means choosing the right tool for the job at hand rather than forcing a framework where it doesn't fit.
- Avoiding "Faux Agile": While it encourages flexibility, it isn't an excuse to be sloppy. It's about making intentional, disciplined choices that lead to better results.

A great example of this is DA’s support for multiple lifecycles. While some frameworks insist on two-week sprints, DA pragmatically acknowledges that some teams might thrive better on a Continuous Delivery or Task-based flow.
