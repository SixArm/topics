# Agile Principle 11: Self-organizing

Question: According to Agile Principle 11, what is the primary reason why the best architectures, requirements, and designs emerge from self-organizing teams?

- [ ] Self-organizing teams can work without any guidance or organizational alignment
- [ ] External architects and managers are not needed in Agile development
- [ ] Team members closest to the work have the deepest understanding of technical challenges and user needs
- [ ] Self-organization eliminates the need for communication with stakeholders

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Team members closest to the work have the deepest understanding of technical challenges and user needs</p>
  <p>Agile Principle 11 recognizes that developers, testers, and other team members possess the deepest understanding of technical challenges and user needs because they are closest to the actual work. This principle shifts the role of traditional architects and managers from decision-makers to facilitators and coaches, enabling collaborative emergence rather than imposed solutions.
