# After-Action Report (AAR)

Question: What is the primary purpose of an After-Action Report (AAR)?

- [ ] To assign blame for project failures and document individual performance issues
- [ ] To create a legal record of events for potential litigation purposes
- [ ] To identify what worked well, what didn't work well, and recommend improvements for the future
- [ ] To generate budget reports and track financial expenditures for completed projects

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To identify what worked well, what didn't work well, and recommend improvements for the future</p>
  <p>An AAR is a structured review conducted after an event or project is completed.
