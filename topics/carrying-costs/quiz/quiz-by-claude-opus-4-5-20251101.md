# Carrying costs

Question: What are carrying costs (also known as holding costs) in inventory management?

- [ ] The one-time expenses incurred when purchasing new inventory from suppliers
- [ ] The ongoing expenses a company incurs to hold and maintain its inventory over time
- [ ] The costs associated with shipping products to customers
- [ ] The fees charged by banks for business transaction processing

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>The ongoing expenses a company incurs to hold and maintain its inventory over time</p>
  <p>Carrying costs include storage costs, insurance, maintenance, taxes, and opportunity cost of capital tied up in inventory. These can be significant expenses, particularly for businesses with large or slow-moving inventory. Companies can reduce carrying costs by improving inventory management practices and negotiating better supplier terms.</p>
</details>
