# Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX)

Question: What is a key requirement that the Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX) places on public companies regarding financial reporting?

- [ ] Companies must outsource all financial auditing to government agencies
- [ ] Companies must establish and maintain internal controls over financial reporting
- [ ] Companies must eliminate their audit committees to reduce conflicts of interest
- [ ] Companies must report only to shareholders, not to the SEC

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Companies must establish and maintain internal controls over financial reporting</p>
  <p>SOX requires public companies to establish and maintain internal controls over financial reporting to ensure the accuracy and reliability of financial statements. This is one of the most significant requirements for technology professionals, as it often involves implementing systems, access controls, audit trails, and automated processes to ensure data integrity and compliance. The CEO and CFO must also certify the accuracy of financial statements, making robust internal controls essential.</p>
</details>
