# Runbook

Question: What is the primary purpose of a runbook in an organization?

- [ ] To track employee performance metrics and attendance records
- [ ] To provide detailed instructions for handling operational tasks, incidents, and processes
- [ ] To store financial data and budget allocations for IT projects
- [ ] To document marketing strategies and customer outreach campaigns

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To provide detailed instructions for handling operational tasks, incidents, and processes</p>
  <p>A runbook (also known as an operations manual or playbook) serves as a reference guide that provides step-by-step instructions for the operations team to follow when managing day-to-day operations, troubleshooting issues, or responding to incidents. Key benefits include consistency, efficiency, knowledge transfer, standardization, and faster incident response and disaster recovery.</p>
</details>
