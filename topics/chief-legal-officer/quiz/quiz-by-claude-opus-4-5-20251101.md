# Chief Legal Officer (CLO)

Question: What is the primary reporting structure for a Chief Legal Officer (CLO) in a company?

- [ ] Reports to the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) and focuses mainly on budget-related legal matters
- [ ] Reports to the Human Resources department and handles only employment law
- [ ] Reports directly to the CEO or board of directors as part of senior management
- [ ] Reports to the Chief Technology Officer (CTO) and manages technology contracts exclusively

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Reports directly to the CEO or board of directors as part of senior management</p>
  <p>The CLO is a top-level executive who is part of the senior management team and reports directly to the CEO or board of directors. This positioning reflects the strategic importance of legal oversight across all company operations, including advising leadership on mergers and acquisitions, ensuring regulatory compliance, managing intellectual property, and overseeing litigation strategy.</p>
</details>
