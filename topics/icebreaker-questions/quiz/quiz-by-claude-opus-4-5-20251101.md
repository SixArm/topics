# Icebreaker questions

Question: What is the primary purpose of using icebreaker questions in a professional team setting?

- [ ] To evaluate team members' technical knowledge and skills
- [ ] To help people feel more comfortable and promote open communication in a group
- [ ] To identify the most experienced person in the group
- [ ] To establish a formal hierarchy among team members

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To help people feel more comfortable and promote open communication in a group</p>
  <p>Icebreaker questions are designed to help people connect and feel more relaxed in new or unfamiliar group settings. Their purpose is to create a sense of camaraderie and encourage open communication among group members, making them particularly valuable for technology teams during onboarding, retrospectives, or when forming new project teams.</p>
</details>
