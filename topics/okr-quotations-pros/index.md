# OKR quotations: pros

"Good OKRs provide measurement and alignment. Good OKRs are designed to be able
to connect up the org chart. Staff should be able to explain how their
contribution serves the mission, rather than their own insular fiefdom. And if
they can't, it might indicate they're not doing what the rest of the org is
planning around!"

"OKRs make it apparent who gets stuff done, who doesn't, and how overloaded some
folks are. and who needs help understanding their jobs."

"In the realm of formal communcations, OKRs are a particularly low-friction,
low-cost-of-adoption hack. 10/10 will use again."

"OKRs, KPIs, etc are often challenging for orgs, teams, and individuals that are
poorly trained. If people are poorly trained, they will find it challenging,
even counter-productive, to use them. The end result is nobody uses it right,
and the company suffers. And it's complex enough that a pithy blog post isn't
going to teach you how to do it right. Managers and execs must train staff."

"OKRs made our employees happier, because it served as a “more abstract” form of
feedback. Before, they were receiving feedback, but it was too specific and
narrow. Things like “this class can be refactored to X pattern” or “You missed a
few unit tests covering X Y Z cases.” But when we started tracking things like
time to ship a new feature, bugs per implemented feature, hard deadlines for
certain product releases, etc. it really helped to give some better feedback."

"One engineering team objective is efficiency. The way to achieve that? By
refactoring code, paying off tech debt, and introducing automation (like CI, CD,
etc). These can be all represented with OKRs."

"Security is an actual objective at Google. Supporting key results would be
things like performing a certain number of audits, conducting pen tests, code
reviews, etc."

"Contradictory goals is the single biggest thing that trips up organizations
which have grown. If whole divisions aren't aligned then it doesn't really
matter how well your team performs because the lowest common denominator
division becomes the limit."

"You simply write down what you're planning on doing this quarter in a
prioritized list. The purpose is to broadcast your view of your priorities, so
interested parties can ask that they be adjusted. At the end of the quarter you
look back and review what you actually accomplished."

"OKRs put goals one place. The process to determine what the OKRs are ensure
that everyone's on the same page about the objectives. People in an organization
want to feel like they know what they're working towards. When they don't know,
they get stressed out and unproductive."

"At the beginning of the quarter, we defined what success looked like for that
new product and came up with the key performance indicators for it. As a
developer, it was really fulfilling to take time to really think through what it
would take to build a truly quality product."

"Figuring out why you do what you do and how you measure the success of that in
a way that leads you to the right result IS hard but extremely valuable work. It
forces you to deeply understand the problem you're solving and the challenges
you face."

"There is a peace that comes with knowing what you are working on is aligned
with the company, and that management has good visibility into how you are
doing. If you take your job seriously, keep up with it."

"OKRs make sense in terms of a complex system, so required reading is Donella
Meadows’ Thinking in Systems. Understand that for some things to change,
everything must change."

"OKRs inspire a conversation about how the company should really be spending its
time and resources. What are the ultimate goals? What are the real
opportunities? What's the dream by investing in initiatives? Once you are
leading an organization or a team, it behooves you to take this type of planning
seriously."

"One of the main benefits of OKRs in my opinion is helping each person
understand where they fit into the big objective. Many people comment about
their desire for ownership of their work. OKRs can play a role in describing a
person's ownership."

"The most important part of OKRs isn’t the metrics, it’s the understanding that
the team has been tasked with solving some problem without being told how to
solve it. The team gets to own the solutions that drive the metric. This is far
better than just being a feature factory that cranks out task tickets."

"Improving SLA metrics are excellent OKRs since they are easily measurable and
are grease to the orgs cogs."

"The performance and communication improvements when you get it right are
astonishing. When you find a place where people get it right, you'll love it."

"OKRs in general makes it much easier for managers to do their job, and you
really want your managers to do their job properly."

"The times I've seen OKRs and metrics used effectively, they met two criteria.
(1) it was always a metric closely aligned with what customers want and which
drives revenue for the company; and (2) it didn't just impose a requirement to
do work, it also provided political cover for not doing competing work."

"Objectives are always inevitably a thing. OKRs make the implicit explicit."

"The big value add from OKRs is about making your goals and work easily
understandable outside your team and even your org. Start with the question of
what you want to make visible to others, then craft your OKRs to highlight that."

"You have an infinite amount of “could do” and a limited amount of “must do”.
OKRs are how you set priorities, and how you resolve the conflicts."
