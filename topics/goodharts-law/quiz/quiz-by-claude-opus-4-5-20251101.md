# Goodhart's Law

Question: What does Goodhart's Law state about metrics and their effectiveness?

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure</p>
  <p>Goodhart's Law, originally formulated by British economist Charles Goodhart, warns that once a metric is used as a target for decision-making or incentives, people will optimize for that specific metric rather than the underlying goal it was meant to represent. This often leads to gaming, manipulation, or unintended consequences.
