# Disciplined Agile principle 2: Be awesome

The second principle of Disciplined Agile states: "Build high-performing, motivated teams where individuals are supported to be their best and continuously improve." This is the people-first principle, recognizing that agile success depends not just on processes but on the humans executing them.

Being awesome means creating workplaces where people thrive. Organizations must establish psychological safety so team members feel comfortable taking risks, admitting mistakes, and raising concerns without fear of retribution. When people feel safe, they experiment more freely and surface problems early, which directly improves delivery outcomes.

The principle calls for developing T-shaped individuals who combine deep expertise in one discipline with enough breadth to collaborate effectively across the team. This skill diversity reduces bottlenecks, improves knowledge sharing, and makes teams more resilient when priorities shift or members are unavailable.

Continuous improvement is central to being awesome. Disciplined Agile embraces the Kaizen mindset, giving teams both the time and authority to experiment with their Way of Working. Rather than prescribing a fixed process, teams evolve their practices based on what they learn. This principle drives Guided Continuous Improvement, the DA technique that uses the toolkit to identify the most promising next steps for team growth.

Joy at work matters. Motivated people who take pride in their craft produce higher quality results and better business outcomes. This is not sentimentality; it is a practical recognition that engagement correlates directly with performance, retention, and innovation.

For technology professionals, this principle reinforces that investing in team culture, individual growth, and working conditions is not optional overhead. It is a core driver of sustainable, high-quality delivery.
