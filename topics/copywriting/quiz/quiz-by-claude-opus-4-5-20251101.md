# Copywriting

Question: What is the primary purpose of a Call-to-Action (CTA) in copywriting?

- [ ] To showcase the writer's vocabulary and linguistic abilities
- [ ] To provide detailed technical specifications of a product
- [ ] To prompt the reader to take a specific desired action
- [ ] To summarize all the content presented in the copy

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To prompt the reader to take a specific desired action</p>
  <p>A Call-to-Action (CTA) is a clear, compelling prompt that directs readers to take a specific action, such as signing up for a service, making a purchase, or sharing content. CTAs are essential in copywriting because they convert reader interest into measurable outcomes, directly supporting the business goal of the copy.</p>
</details>
