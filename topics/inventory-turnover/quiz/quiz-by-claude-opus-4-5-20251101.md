# Inventory turnover

Question: What does a higher inventory turnover ratio indicate about a company's operations?

- [ ] The company has excessive carrying costs and obsolescence risk
- [ ] The company is selling inventory more slowly than competitors
- [ ] The company is managing its inventory more efficiently
- [ ] The company needs to increase its average inventory levels

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>The company is managing its inventory more efficiently</p>
  <p>A higher inventory turnover ratio indicates that a company is selling its inventory more quickly, which means it is managing inventory more efficiently. This results in lower carrying costs, reduced risk of inventory obsolescence, and increased cash flow. For technology professionals, understanding this metric is valuable when building inventory management systems, supply chain software, or business intelligence dashboards.</p>
</details>
