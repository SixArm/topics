# One-on-one meeting

Question: What is the primary purpose of regular one-on-one meetings between a manager and an employee?

- [ ] To provide a formal setting for annual performance reviews
- [ ] To ensure ongoing communication and discuss work-related topics in a private, confidential setting
- [ ] To assign new projects and delegate tasks to employees
- [ ] To document employee mistakes for HR records

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To ensure ongoing communication and discuss work-related topics in a private, confidential setting</p>
  <p>One-on-one meetings are designed to maintain regular, private communication between managers and employees. They provide a confidential space to discuss project updates, give and receive feedback, address concerns, and build a strong working relationship. This ongoing dialogue helps identify issues early and improves employee engagement and job satisfaction.</p>
</details>
