# The Design of Everyday Things by Donald Norman

Question: According to Donald Norman, what is the primary focus of human-centered design?

- [ ] Maximizing the aesthetic appeal and visual complexity of products
- [ ] Prioritizing the needs, capabilities, and limitations of users
- [ ] Reducing manufacturing costs through simplified components
- [ ] Implementing the latest technological innovations regardless of usability

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Prioritizing the needs, capabilities, and limitations of users</p>
  <p>Norman emphasizes that human-centered design is an approach that puts users first, requiring empathy and understanding of user behavior and mental models when designing for usability. This principle underlies all other design concepts in the book, including discoverability, affordances, conceptual models, and error handling.</p>
</details>
