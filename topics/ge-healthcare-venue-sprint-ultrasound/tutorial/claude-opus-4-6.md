# GE HealthCare Venue Sprint Ultrasound

The GE HealthCare Venue Sprint is a tablet-based point-of-care ultrasound (POCUS) system launched in late 2024. It targets clinicians who need high-quality diagnostic imaging in mobile, space-constrained environments. Built around a wireless architecture and AI-assisted software, the Venue Sprint represents GE HealthCare's push to make advanced ultrasound imaging practical at the bedside, in the field, and during patient transport.

## Device Overview

The Venue Sprint uses a compact, all-in-one tablet console paired with Vscan Air wireless dual-probes. This wireless architecture eliminates cable tethering, enabling clinicians to scan freely while moving between patients or locations. The console includes a built-in probe charger that supports continuous use without returning to a separate docking station, and the device is designed for easy cleaning to meet infection control requirements in acute care environments.

The system form factor is a deliberate departure from cart-based ultrasound systems. By consolidating the display, processor, and probe charging into a single handheld tablet, GE HealthCare reduces the physical footprint to a size suitable for crowded emergency departments, ambulances, and field clinics.

## AI-Enabled Software and Automation

The Venue Sprint runs the same AI-enabled software platform as the larger Venue family devices, including the Venue Go and Venue Fit. Key automated tools include:

- **Bladder volume calculation** with reference guidance, reducing the need for manual measurement and catheterization decisions.
- **Real-time arterial flow volume measurement**, providing hemodynamic data at the bedside without requiring a vascular lab.
- **Venue Coach MSK**, which overlays anatomy markups and video tutorials during musculoskeletal scans, guiding less experienced operators through correct probe positioning and anatomical identification.

These features lower the skill barrier for point-of-care imaging, improve measurement consistency across operators with varying experience levels, and reduce the time required to obtain clinically actionable results.

## Clinical Applications

The Venue Sprint supports imaging workflows across several specialties:

- **Emergency and critical care**: Rapid bedside assessments for trauma triage, cardiac evaluation, lung imaging, and FAST exams in time-sensitive scenarios.
- **Musculoskeletal imaging**: The wireless curved and linear dual-probes enable tendon, joint, and soft tissue evaluation without repositioning bulky equipment, which is particularly valuable in sports medicine and orthopedic clinics.
- **Vascular access**: Real-time ultrasound guidance for central line placement, peripheral IV insertion, and arterial access, improving first-attempt success rates and reducing complications.
- **Superficial imaging**: Support for aesthetic procedures and dermatologic assessments where high-frequency, near-field resolution is required.

## Wireless Probe Architecture

The Vscan Air wireless probes are a central differentiator for the Venue Sprint. Each probe contains both a curved array and a linear array transducer in a single housing, which means a clinician can switch between deep abdominal imaging and superficial vascular or musculoskeletal imaging without physically changing probes.

| Comparison | Traditional Cart-Based | Venue Sprint |
|---|---|---|
| Probe connection | Wired, cable-tethered | Wireless (Vscan Air) |
| Probe switching | Manual swap of physical probes | Dual-probe, single housing |
| Portability | Limited by cart and cables | Fully mobile tablet |
| Charging | Separate dock or wall outlet | Built-in console charger |
| Setup time | Minutes (boot, connect, calibrate) | Seconds (power on, scan) |

## Data Connectivity and Reporting

Data connectivity integrates with GE HealthCare's ViewPoint 6 reporting software, enabling structured image archiving and clinical documentation directly from the device. This closes the loop between bedside imaging and electronic health record (EHR) systems. Benefits include:

- **Structured reporting**: Images and measurements flow into standardized templates, reducing free-text variability.
- **Audit traceability**: Every scan is logged with metadata (operator, timestamp, patient context), supporting quality assurance and accreditation workflows.
- **Reduced manual transcription**: Direct integration eliminates the error-prone step of manually entering ultrasound findings into the EHR.

For institutions using DICOM-compliant PACS infrastructure, the Venue Sprint fits into existing radiology archiving workflows without requiring a separate image management layer.

## Summary

The GE HealthCare Venue Sprint is a tablet-form-factor point-of-care ultrasound system that pairs wireless Vscan Air dual-probes with AI-enabled imaging software to deliver portable, high-quality diagnostic imaging. Its wireless architecture, built-in probe charging, automated measurement tools, and direct EHR integration via ViewPoint 6 make it a practical choice for emergency departments, intensive care units, transport teams, and ambulatory settings where mobility, speed, and workflow integration are critical requirements.
