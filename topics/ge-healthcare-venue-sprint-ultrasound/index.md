# GE HealthCare Venue Sprint Ultrasound

The GE HealthCare Venue Sprint is a tablet-based point-of-care ultrasound (POCUS) system launched in late 2024. It targets clinicians who need high-quality diagnostic imaging in mobile, space-constrained settings such as emergency departments, intensive care units, and medical transport vehicles.

The Venue Sprint uses a compact, all-in-one tablet console paired with Vscan Air wireless dual-probes. This wireless architecture eliminates cable tethering, allowing clinicians to scan freely while moving between patients or locations. The console includes a built-in probe charger for continuous use without returning to a docking station, and the device is designed for easy cleaning to meet infection control requirements in acute care environments.

The system runs the same AI-enabled software platform as the larger Venue family devices. Automated tools include bladder volume calculation with reference guidance, real-time arterial flow volume measurement, and Venue Coach MSK, which overlays anatomy markups and video tutorials during musculoskeletal scans. These features reduce the skill barrier for point-of-care imaging and improve consistency across operators with varying experience levels.

Clinical applications span several specialties. In emergency and critical care, it supports rapid bedside assessments and triage. For musculoskeletal work, the wireless curved and linear dual-probes enable tendon and joint evaluation without repositioning bulky equipment. Vascular access workflows provide real-time guidance for central line and peripheral IV placement. The system also supports superficial imaging for aesthetic procedures.

Data connectivity integrates with GE HealthCare's ViewPoint 6 reporting software, enabling structured image archiving and clinical documentation directly from the device. This closes the loop between bedside imaging and electronic health record systems, reducing manual transcription and improving audit traceability.
