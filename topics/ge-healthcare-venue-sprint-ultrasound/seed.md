# GE HealthCare Venue Sprint Ultrasound

The Venue Sprint is a highly portable, tablet-based point-of-care ultrasound (POCUS) system from GE HealthCare launched in late 2024. It is designed for maximum mobility, featuring a streamlined console that integrates with wireless probes to provide high-quality imaging in fast-paced environments like emergency rooms, ICUs, and medical transport. 

Key Features
- Portability & Design: A compact, all-in-one tablet console that is easy to clean and designed to fit into tight clinical spaces.
- Wireless Integration: Specifically designed to work with Vscan Air wireless dual-probes, allowing clinicians to scan without being tethered by wires.
- AI-Enabled Tools: Utilizes the same powerful software found in the larger Venue family systems, offering automated clinical tools for:
- Bladder Volume: Automatically calculates bladder volume with reference guidance.
- Venue Coach MSK: Provides anatomy markups and video tutorials to assist in musculoskeletal scans.
- Auto Volume Flow: Real-time arterial flow volume calculations.
- Workflow Efficiency: Features a built-in charger for on-the-go use and seamless data connectivity with ViewPoint 6 reporting software. 

The Venue Sprint is built for versatility across several medical specialties: 
- Emergency & Critical Care: Quick assessments at the bedside or in ambulances.
- Musculoskeletal (MSK): Assessment of tendons and joints using wireless dual-probes (curved and linear).
- Vascular Access: Guidance for central lines and peripheral access.
- Aesthetics: Superficial imaging for procedures like chin and jawline fillers.
