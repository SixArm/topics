# Disciplined Agile promises

In Disciplined Agile (DA), the Promises are commitments that team members make to one another and to the broader organization. If the DA Principles represent the "Why" behind disciplined delivery, the Promises represent the "How" — a social contract that turns philosophy into daily behavior.

DA defines seven Promises that guide how teams operate.

Create Psychological Safety and Trust. Teams commit to building an environment where people can speak up, experiment, and fail without fear. Trust is earned through consistent action and must be actively maintained.

Collaborate Proactively. Team members reach out across boundaries without waiting to be asked. They share knowledge and offer help whenever it moves the mission forward.

Make All Work and Workflow Visible. Teams use visual management techniques such as Kanban boards to expose bottlenecks, hidden tasks, and the true state of work so everyone can optimize flow.

Keep Workloads Within Capacity. Teams limit work in progress to prevent overload. Overburdened teams make more mistakes and burn out, so realistic capacity management is a deliberate commitment.

Accelerate Value Realization. The focus stays on finishing work that delivers value quickly rather than staying busy with low-priority items. Speed to market matters more than activity.

Be Transparent. Teams commit to honest reporting about project state, including risks, delays, and failures. This replaces "status report green" theater with reality-based decision making.

Improve Continuously. Teams refuse to accept the status quo. They constantly seek better ways of working, using the DA toolkit to guide their experiments and adaptations.

Together, these seven promises bind a team into a functioning social contract. They bridge the gap between abstract principles and concrete team behavior, giving every member a shared standard for accountability and collaboration.
