# The ball is in your court

Question: What does the idiom "the ball is in your court" signify in a business or negotiation context?

- [ ] The discussion has reached a deadlock and requires mediation
- [ ] It is now your turn to take action or make a decision
- [ ] The project timeline has been extended indefinitely
- [ ] Both parties must collaborate simultaneously on the next step

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>It is now your turn to take action or make a decision</p>
  <p>This idiom, originating from tennis where players take turns hitting the ball, indicates that responsibility for the next step or decision lies with the person being addressed. It is a polite way to shift responsibility and create a sense of urgency for that person to respond or act.</p>
</details>
