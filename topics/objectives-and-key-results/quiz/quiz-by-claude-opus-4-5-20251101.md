# Objectives and Key Results (OKRs)

Question: What is the primary purpose of defining "Key Results" in the OKR framework?

- [ ] To create high-level aspirational goals for the organization
- [ ] To establish specific, measurable outcomes that indicate progress toward objectives
- [ ] To assign individual tasks and daily activities to team members
- [ ] To document the company's mission and vision statements

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To establish specific, measurable outcomes that indicate progress toward objectives</p>
  <p>Key Results are the specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and timely (SMART) outcomes that a company wants to achieve in order to reach its objectives. While objectives are high-level goals, key results provide the concrete, quantifiable measures that indicate whether those objectives are being met. This distinction is fundamental to the OKR frameworkâ€”objectives define what you want to accomplish, and key results define how you will know you've accomplished it.</p>
