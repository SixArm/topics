# Five Whys analysis

Question: What is the primary objective of Five Whys analysis?

- [ ] To document all symptoms of a problem for future reference
- [ ] To assign responsibility for problems to specific team members
- [ ] To identify the root cause of a problem by repeatedly asking "why"
- [ ] To generate five different solutions for a single problem

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To identify the root cause of a problem by repeatedly asking "why"</p>
  <p>Five Whys analysis is a problem-solving technique that involves asking "why" five times to drill down from symptoms to underlying causes. By identifying the root cause rather than just addressing symptoms, teams can develop effective solutions that prevent the same problem from recurring in the future.</p>
</details>
