# Get on the front foot

Question: What does the phrase "get on the front foot" mean in a business context?

- [ ] Wait for problems to occur before addressing them
- [ ] Let someone else take the leadership role
- [ ] Take a proactive approach by anticipating problems or opportunities and acting before they become urgent
- [ ] React quickly after receiving customer complaints

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Take a proactive approach by anticipating problems or opportunities and acting before they become urgent</p>
  <p>In a business context, "get on the front foot" means being proactive rather than reactive. It involves anticipating potential problems or opportunities and taking action before they become urgent or critical, such as reaching out to customers to address concerns before complaints arise. The phrase originates from sports like football (soccer), where attacking players control the pace and direction of the game.</p>
</details>
