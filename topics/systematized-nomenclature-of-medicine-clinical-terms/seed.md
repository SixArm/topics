# Systematized Nomenclature of Medicine - Clinical Terms (SNOMED CT) 

Systematized Nomenclature of Medicine - Clinical Terms (SNOMED CT) is the
world's most comprehensive, multilingual clinical healthcare terminology,
providing a standardized vocabulary for electronic health records. It enables
consistent recording, exchanging, and analysis of clinical data—including
diagnoses, symptoms, and procedures—across all care settings, improving patient
care.

## Key Aspects of SNOMED CT

Comprehensive Coverage: Includes over 350,000 concepts, covering diseases,
clinical findings, procedures, medications, and anatomy.

Structure: It is structured with hierarchies and relationships (e.g., "viral
pneumonia" is a "pneumonia") allowing for sophisticated data analysis and
clinical decision support.

Interoperability: Used internationally to ensure clinical information can be
shared seamlessly between different health systems.

Updates: Maintained by SNOMED International, with regular updates to reflect
medical advancements.

Usage: It is essential for mapping clinical data to other standards, such as
ICD-10 for coding.
