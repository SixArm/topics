# Get out of the building

"Get out of the building" (GOOB) is a mantra coined by Steve Blank, a key figure in the Lean Startup movement. It challenges the dangerous assumption that you already know what your customers want. In Blank's view, there are no facts inside the building, only opinions. To build a product people actually want, you must leave your office and observe potential users in their real-world environment to validate your business hypotheses.

In a startup or product team, Agile provides the iterative building process, while Customer Development through GOOB provides the "why" and "for whom." Customer Development is ineffective if the engineering team uses slow, rigid waterfall methods. Agile allows teams to take insights gained from outside the building and quickly pivot or iterate the product. The goal is not just gathering data or running focus groups. You are looking for deep insight into the customer's daily routine, pain points, and frustrations so you can build a Minimum Viable Product that solves a real problem.

Steve Blank's framework for getting out of the building involves four phases. Customer Discovery is where founders test their initial hypotheses about customer problems and needs by talking to real people. Customer Validation proves the business model is scalable by confirming customers will actually pay for the solution. Customer Creation scales the company by driving end-user demand through marketing and sales. Company Building transitions the organization from a learning-focused startup to a formal structure designed for execution.

The core lesson for technology professionals is clear: validate assumptions with real users before investing engineering effort. Ship learning before shipping code.
