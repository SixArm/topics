# Heart rate variability RMSSD

Heart rate variability (HRV) RMSSD stands for Root Mean Square of Successive Differences. It quantifies the variation in time intervals between consecutive heartbeats, calculated in milliseconds. RMSSD specifically reflects parasympathetic nervous system activity, the "rest and digest" branch of the autonomic nervous system, making it the preferred metric for assessing short-term recovery, stress levels, and autonomic balance.

RMSSD is computed by taking the differences between each pair of successive inter-beat intervals, squaring those differences, averaging them, and then taking the square root. This approach emphasizes beat-to-beat changes rather than long-term trends, which makes it particularly sensitive to rapid shifts in autonomic tone. A higher RMSSD generally indicates stronger parasympathetic influence, better cardiovascular recovery, and greater physiological resilience. A lower RMSSD suggests elevated stress, fatigue, or insufficient recovery.

For healthy adults, typical RMSSD values range from approximately 27 to 72 milliseconds, though individual baselines vary considerably based on age, sex, and fitness level. HRV naturally declines with age, so RMSSD values tend to decrease over time. Athletes and highly fit individuals often exhibit higher baseline values compared to sedentary populations.

RMSSD is distinct from SDNN, another common HRV metric. SDNN captures overall autonomic variability across longer recording periods, while RMSSD isolates short-term, parasympathetically-driven fluctuations. RMSSD is mathematically equivalent to SD1 in Poincare plot analysis, reinforcing its role as a measure of rapid heart rate changes.

Consumer wearables and clinical systems widely use RMSSD to guide training load decisions, monitor recovery readiness, and detect early signs of overtraining or illness. Improving RMSSD generally involves consistent exercise, adequate sleep, proper hydration, balanced nutrition, and managing stress. Tracking RMSSD trends over weeks provides more actionable insight than any single measurement.
