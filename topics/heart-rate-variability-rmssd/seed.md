# Heart rate variability RMSSD

Heart rate variability (HRV) Root Mean Square of Successive Differences (RMSSD)
is a metric measuring the variation in time between consecutive heartbeats. It
reflects parasympathetic nervous system activity ("rest and digest") and is the
preferred marker for monitoring short-term recovery, stress, and autonomic
nervous system integrity.Key RMSSD Details:Definition: The root mean square of
successive differences between normal heartbeats, calculated in milliseconds
(ms).Significance: Higher RMSSD indicates better recovery and fitness, while
lower values often signify stress, fatigue, or illness.Normal Ranges: For
healthy adults, RMSSD typically ranges from 27 to 72 ms, though it varies
significantly based on age, sex, and fitness level.Age Factor: HRV naturally
decreases with age, causing typical RMSSD values to drop over time.Common Use:
Widely used by wearables and professionals to assess training load and readiness
to perform.RMSSD vs. Other HRV MetricsRMSSD vs. SDNN: RMSSD is better for
measuring short-term, parasympathetic-driven variability, while SDNN reflects
overall autonomic activity.RMSSD vs. SD1: RMSSD is mathematically identical to
SD1, a component of Poincaré plot analysis.How to Improve RMSSDRegular Exercise:
Consistent physical activity can increase your baseline HRV.Recovery: Ensuring
adequate sleep and rest.Lifestyle Factors: Proper hydration, balanced nutrition,
and minimizing excessive stress and alcohol consumption.
