# Statistical analysis

Question: What is the primary distinction between descriptive statistics and inferential statistics?

- [ ] Descriptive statistics uses surveys while inferential statistics uses experiments
- [ ] Descriptive statistics summarizes data features, while inferential statistics draws conclusions about a population from a sample
- [ ] Descriptive statistics analyzes qualitative data while inferential statistics analyzes quantitative data
- [ ] Descriptive statistics is used in business while inferential statistics is used in science

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Descriptive statistics summarizes data features, while inferential statistics draws conclusions about a population from a sample</p>
  <p>Descriptive statistics focuses on summarizing and describing the main features of data (such as mean, median, mode, and standard deviation), whereas inferential statistics involves making inferences or drawing conclusions about a larger population based on data from a sample. Understanding this distinction is fundamental for technology professionals who need to analyze data correctlyâ€”knowing when to simply describe what the data shows versus when to make broader predictions or generalizations.</p>
</details>
