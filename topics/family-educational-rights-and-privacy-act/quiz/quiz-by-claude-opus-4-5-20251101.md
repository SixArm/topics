# Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA)

Question: What is the primary consequence for educational institutions that violate FERPA?

- [ ] Criminal prosecution of school administrators
- [ ] Mandatory data breach notification to all affected students
- [ ] Loss of federal funding
- [ ] Automatic accreditation revocation

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Loss of federal funding</p>
  <p>FERPA applies to educational institutions that receive federal funding, and violations can result in the loss of that federal funding. This financial consequence serves as the primary enforcement mechanism for the law, along with potential reputational damage and legal liability. For technology professionals working with educational systems, understanding this consequence emphasizes the importance of properly implementing data privacy controls for student records.</p>
</details>
