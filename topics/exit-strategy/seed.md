# Exit strategy

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exit_strategy

An exit strategy is a planned, strategic method for a business owner, investor, or venture to transition out of a business or investment to maximize value or minimize losses. Common strategies include selling to another company (acquisition), a merger, initial public offering (IPO), or management buyout. Early planning ensures a smoother transition and higher valuation.

Purpose: To exit after achieving goals or to limit damage from failure.

Timing: Should be considered from the beginning of the venture.

Why an Exit Strategy is Essential

- Maximizing Value: A well-planned exit helps avoid leaving money on the table.

- Risk Management: Protects against market volatility and emotional decision-making, especially in investing.

- Business Continuity: Ensures the company can thrive even after the founder leaves.

- Preparation: Involves organizing financial records, improving operational efficiency, and, on average, taking four to six months for the process.

Common Methods:

- Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A): Selling to a competitor or larger company is a common, often profitable route.

- IPO: Taking the company public, usually for high-growth startups.

- Management Buyout (MBO): Selling to the existing management team.

- Passing on the Business: Family succession or passing to partners.

- Liquidation: Closing down, selling assets, and paying off debts.
