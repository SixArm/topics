# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Question: What criteria should Key Performance Indicators meet to be effective in measuring organizational performance?

- [ ] Broad, flexible, aspirational, long-term, and inspirational (BFALI)
- [ ] Quick, universal, easy, simple, and trackable (QUEST)
- [ ] Specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and timely (SMART)
- [ ] Detailed, extensive, comprehensive, optimized, and refined (DECOR)

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and timely (SMART)</p>
  <p>KPIs should follow the SMART criteria to be effective. They need to be Specific (clearly defined), Measurable (quantifiable), Achievable (realistic), Relevant (aligned with organizational goals), and Timely (with a defined timeframe). This framework ensures KPIs provide actionable insights and can be tracked meaningfully over time.</p>
</details>
