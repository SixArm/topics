# United States National Drug Code (US NDC)

The United States National Drug Code (NDC) is a unique, three-segment identifier assigned to human drugs in the United States by the Food and Drug Administration. It serves as a universal product identifier, appearing on the labels of all prescription and over-the-counter (OTC) medications.

## Structure and Components

The code is divided into three distinct segments:

- Labeler Code: Assigned by the [FDA](https://www.fda.gov/drugs/drug-approvals-and-databases/national-drug-code-directory), it identifies the manufacturer, repackager, or distributor.
- Product Code: Assigned by the company, it identifies a specific strength, dosage form, and formulation (e.g., 20 mg capsule).
- Package Code: Assigned by the company, it identifies the specific package size and type (e.g., bottle of 100).

## Format and Variations

- 10-Digit (FDA Standard): Currently, the FDA assigns codes in 10-digit formats using configurations like 4-4-2, 5-3-2, or 5-4-1.
- 11-Digit (Reimbursement Standard): For billing and claims (such as under [HIPAA](https://www.techtarget.com/searchhealthit/definition/HIPAA)), a 11-digit format (5-4-2) is used. This often requires adding a leading zero to one of the segments to maintain consistency across software systems.

## Major Upcoming Transition: 12-Digit NDC

To prevent running out of available labeler codes, the [FDA](https://www.fda.gov/) has finalized a rule to move to a uniform 12-digit format.

- Standardized Structure: The new format will be a fixed 6-4-2 configuration (6-digit labeler, 4-digit product, 2-digit package).
- Timeline:
- Now through 6 March 2033: The FDA continues to assign 10-digit codes while the industry prepares.
  - 7 March 2033: The effective date when the FDA will begin assigning only 12-digit NDCs and existing 10-digit codes will be converted in official records.
  - By 7 March 2036: All product labels must display the 12-digit format to avoid potential regulatory action.

## Searching the Directory

The FDA maintains the National Drug Code Directory, a searchable database updated daily that includes active finished and unfinished drugs. You can also verify labeling through the [FDALabel database](https://www.fda.gov/science-research/bioinformatics-tools/fdalabel-full-text-search-drug-product-labeling).
