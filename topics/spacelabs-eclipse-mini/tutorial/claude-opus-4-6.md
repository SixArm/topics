# Spacelabs Eclipse Mini

The Spacelabs Eclipse Mini is a wireless, patch-based Holter recorder manufactured by Spacelabs Healthcare, designed for extended continuous cardiac monitoring of up to 30 days. It represents a significant advancement in ambulatory electrocardiography, targeting the detection of infrequent arrhythmias such as atrial fibrillation, supraventricular tachycardia, and bradycardia episodes. The device is suitable for both paediatric and adult patients in clinical or home settings, and its compact, self-contained design addresses many of the compliance and usability limitations associated with traditional Holter monitoring systems.

## Clinical Purpose and Indications

The primary clinical rationale for extended cardiac monitoring is the detection of transient or sporadic cardiac events that shorter-duration monitors would miss. A standard 24- or 48-hour Holter recording captures only a narrow window, and many arrhythmias occur infrequently enough that they fall outside that window entirely. The Eclipse Mini addresses this gap by recording high-resolution, 3-channel ECG data continuously for up to 30 days, providing complete disclosure for detailed clinical analysis.

Common indications for extended monitoring with the Eclipse Mini include:

- Evaluation of unexplained syncope or near-syncope
- Detection of paroxysmal atrial fibrillation in patients with cryptogenic stroke
- Assessment of palpitation symptoms that occur infrequently
- Monitoring antiarrhythmic drug efficacy over extended periods
- Post-ablation surveillance for arrhythmia recurrence
- Paediatric arrhythmia evaluation where compliance with traditional monitors is difficult

## Device Design and Physical Characteristics

A key design choice of the Eclipse Mini is the integration of the battery directly into the adhesive electrode patch. This eliminates manual battery insertion and removes the need for external lead wires, simplifying setup for clinicians and reducing points of failure. The result is a self-contained unit that is straightforward to deploy.

Patient compliance is addressed through its physical design. The device weighs under 15 grams and carries an IP67 waterproof rating, allowing patients to shower and maintain normal daily routines without removing or protecting the monitor. This is a practical advantage over traditional Holter systems that constrain patient activity.

## Comparison with Traditional Holter Monitors

The Eclipse Mini differs from conventional Holter monitoring systems in several important respects. Understanding these differences helps clinicians select the appropriate monitoring approach for each patient scenario.

| Attribute | Traditional Holter Monitor | Spacelabs Eclipse Mini |
|---|---|---|
| Typical duration | 24 to 48 hours | Up to 30 days |
| Lead wires | Multiple external wires | None (patch-based) |
| Battery management | User-replaceable batteries | Integrated into patch |
| Water resistance | Generally not waterproof | IP67 waterproof |
| Weight | Typically 100+ grams | Under 15 grams |
| Patient activity impact | Restricts showering, some activities | Minimal lifestyle disruption |
| Setup complexity | Requires lead placement training | Simplified single-patch application |
| Event capture probability | Limited by short duration | Substantially higher due to extended wear |

## Patient Interaction and Symptom Correlation

For symptom correlation, the Eclipse Mini provides a dual-input approach to event marking:

- **Physical button**: A button on the device itself allows patients to mark events at the moment symptoms occur, without requiring any additional technology.
- **Smartphone application**: A linked smartphone app provides an alternative method for marking events, with the added ability to annotate symptoms with descriptive information.

This dual-input design ensures that symptom data is captured regardless of whether the patient has their phone accessible. Accurate symptom correlation is clinically valuable because it allows the reviewing physician to align subjective patient experiences with objective ECG findings, strengthening diagnostic conclusions.

## Data Analysis Workflow

Once the monitoring period concludes, data is downloaded via a standard USB-C cable. The use of USB-C reflects current connectivity standards and simplifies the hardware requirements for data retrieval. Analysis is then performed using one of two compatible Spacelabs software platforms:

- **Spacelabs LifeScreen Pro**: An event screening system designed for rapid review of extended monitoring data, with tools for identifying and categorizing arrhythmia events across the full recording.
- **Pathfinder SL Holter Analysis System**: A comprehensive Holter analysis platform that supports detailed morphology analysis, heart rate variability assessment, and full disclosure review of the accumulated recording data.

Both platforms are designed to handle the large data volumes generated by 30-day continuous recordings, providing clinicians with efficient workflows for reviewing what can amount to several hundred hours of ECG data.

## Deployment Considerations

When integrating the Eclipse Mini into a clinical workflow, several practical factors are worth evaluating:

- **Skin preparation**: Proper electrode site preparation remains important for signal quality, as with any adhesive-based ECG device. Clinicians should follow standard skin preparation protocols to minimize impedance and artifact.
- **Patch adhesion over time**: Extended wear durations of up to 30 days require robust adhesive performance. Patients should be counselled on activities or skin conditions that may compromise adhesion.
- **Data volume management**: A 30-day, 3-channel recording produces a substantial data set. The receiving analysis system and associated storage infrastructure must be provisioned accordingly.
- **Patient education**: While the device is simple to wear, patients benefit from clear instructions on event marking, expected wear duration, and when to contact the clinic if the device becomes dislodged.

## Summary

The Spacelabs Eclipse Mini is a purpose-built extended cardiac monitoring device that combines a 30-day recording capability with a lightweight, waterproof, patch-based form factor requiring no external lead wires or manual battery management. Its design directly addresses the two primary challenges in ambulatory cardiac monitoring: capturing infrequent arrhythmias that escape shorter recording windows, and maintaining patient compliance over the monitoring period. By integrating the battery into the disposable electrode patch, providing dual-method event marking, and supporting data retrieval through USB-C into established analysis platforms such as LifeScreen Pro and Pathfinder SL, the Eclipse Mini offers a clinically practical solution for extended ambulatory electrocardiography across both adult and paediatric populations.
