# Spacelabs Eclipse Mini

The Spacelabs Eclipse Mini is a wireless, patch-based Holter recorder designed for continuous cardiac monitoring for up to 30 days. It targets the detection of infrequent arrhythmias, such as atrial fibrillation, and is suitable for both paediatric and adult patients in clinical or home settings.

The device records high-resolution, 3-channel ECG data across its full monitoring period, providing complete disclosure for detailed clinical analysis. This extended recording window significantly increases the likelihood of capturing transient or sporadic cardiac events that shorter-duration monitors would miss.

A key design choice is the integration of the battery directly into the adhesive electrode patch. This eliminates manual battery insertion and removes the need for external lead wires, simplifying setup for clinicians and reducing points of failure. The result is a self-contained unit that is straightforward to deploy.

Patient compliance is addressed through its physical design. The device weighs under 15 grams and carries an IP67 waterproof rating, allowing patients to shower and maintain normal daily routines without removing or protecting the monitor. This is a practical advantage over traditional Holter systems that constrain patient activity.

For symptom correlation, patients can mark events using a physical button on the device or through a linked smartphone application. This dual-input approach ensures that symptom data is captured regardless of whether the patient has their phone accessible.

Once the monitoring period concludes, data is downloaded via a standard USB-C cable. Analysis is performed using the Spacelabs LifeScreen Pro event screening system or the Pathfinder SL Holter Analysis System, both of which support rapid review of the accumulated recording data.
