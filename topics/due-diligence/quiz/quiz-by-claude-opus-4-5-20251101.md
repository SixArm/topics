# Due Diligence

Question: What is the primary goal of due diligence in a business transaction?

- [ ] To negotiate the lowest possible purchase price
- [ ] To delay the transaction until market conditions improve
- [ ] To provide all relevant information needed to make an informed decision about the transaction
- [ ] To identify reasons to terminate the deal

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To provide all relevant information needed to make an informed decision about the transaction</p>
  <p>Due diligence is a comprehensive investigation that examines financial, legal, operational, and commercial aspects of a company. Its purpose is to assess both benefits and risks, giving the interested party the information they need to decide whether to proceed with the transactionâ€”not simply to find problems or negotiate price.</p>
