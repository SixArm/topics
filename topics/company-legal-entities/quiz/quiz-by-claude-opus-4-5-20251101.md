# Company legal entities

Question: Which company legal entity type allows profits to "pass through" to owners' personal tax returns while still providing limited liability protection?

- [ ] C-Corporation
- [ ] General Partnership
- [ ] Limited Liability Company (LLC)
- [ ] Sole Proprietorship

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Limited Liability Company (LLC)</p>
  <p>An LLC combines the liability protection of a corporation with the tax benefits of a partnership. Unlike a C-Corporation (which pays corporate income tax and then shareholders pay tax on dividends), an LLC is not taxed as a separate entityâ€”instead, profits pass through to the owners who report them on their personal tax returns. Unlike a general partnership, LLC owners have limited liability protection, meaning they are not personally responsible for the business's debts and obligations.</p>
</details>
