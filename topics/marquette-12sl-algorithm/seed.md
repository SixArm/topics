# Marquette 12SL Algorithm

The Marquette 12SL Algorithm is a clinically validated ECG analysis program developed by GE HealthCare that provides automated computer-generated measurements and interpretive support for 12-lead resting ECGs.

## Core Features and Capabilities

The algorithm is designed to act as a "second opinion" for clinicians, assisting in the detection of various cardiac conditions:

- Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS) Tool: Increases sensitivity for detecting ST-Elevated Myocardial Infarction (STEMI) and acute ischemia by weighing ST elevation alongside reciprocal depression.
- Gender and Age-Specific Criteria: Applies unique criteria for adult men and women to improve the sensitivity of MI detection in women under 60. It also includes dedicated pediatric criteria for 12 age groups, from neonates to adolescents.
- QT Analysis: Measures the QT interval from a median complex using global fiducial points across all 12 leads to reduce noise and provide highly accurate, reproducible QTc values.
- Serial Comparison: When used with the MUSE ECG management system, it automatically compares current and previous ECGs for the same patient to detect clinically significant changes.
- Pace Detection: Analyzes artificially paced rhythms at high sampling rates to identify underlying rhythms and the specific chambers being paced.
- Hookup Advisor: A quality check tool that uses a red-yellow-green signal to indicate electrode signal quality and impedance, helping ensure a high-quality trace before acquisition.

## Clinical Application

The algorithm is integrated into a wide range of GE devices, including the MAC 2000, MAC 600, and PC-based CardioSoft systems. While highly accurate (reaching up to 99% accuracy in some metrics), all computer-generated interpretations are intended as aids and must be reviewed and confirmed by a physician.

## Naming

The Marquette 12SL Algorithm is named after Marquette Electronics, the company that developed it before being acquired by General Electric (GE). "12SL" refers to 12-lead Simultaneous analysis, representing its ability to analyze all 12 ECG leads simultaneously to provide diagnostic interpretations.

Key details regarding the name include:

- Origin: Marquette Electronics (based in Milwaukee, WI) developed this seminal ECG analysis program, which became standard in, and often branded as, GE Healthcare technologies.
- 12SL Meaning: Standing for "12-lead Simultaneous," the algorithm was pioneering in interpreting all leads at once, rather than sequentially, for faster, more accurate diagnosis of Acute Coronary Syndromes (ACS).
- Significance: It is widely recognized in cardiology for its high sensitivity and specificity in detecting arrhythmias and myocardial infarction.

Since its introduction in 1980, it has become a industry standard for clinical decision support, consistently refined with input from cardiologists and validated against extensive "gold-standard" databases.
