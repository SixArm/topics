# Definition of Customer (DoC)

The customer is the person or entity that pays for the system, owns the business requirements, and ultimately decides if the product is a success. While they are often confused with "end users," the customer is specifically the one who authorizes the work and realizes the business value.

## Key Characteristics

- The Buyer: They provide the funding and resources for the project.
- The Decision Maker: They have the final say on whether a feature is "valuable" enough to build.
- The Visionary: They define the overarching business goals and "why" the project exists.

## The Customer vs. The End User

The Customer: Wants a return on investment (ROI), competitive advantage, and project completion. (e.g., A hospital administrator buying a new patient portal).

The End User: Wants ease of use, speed, and specific functionality. (e.g., The nurses and doctors using that portal daily).

## How the Customer Fits into the Software Development Lifecycle

Product Owner (Proxy): Because a high-level customer (like a CEO) rarely has time for software development team meetings, the Product Owner acts as their representative, translating their business vision into the Priority Queue.

Acceptance: Only the customer (or their proxy) can officially "accept" a completed user story as meeting the business requirement.

Feedback Loops: Customers can be invited to demonstrations, such as weekly showcases or ongoing research deployments, to see the working software and provide the "thumbs up" or "thumbs down" for the direction of development.
