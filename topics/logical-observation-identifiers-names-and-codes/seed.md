# Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes (LOINC)

Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes (LOINC) is a universal standard for identifying laboratory tests, clinical observations, and diagnostic reports, enabling interoperability between electronic health records (EHRs), labs, and public health authorities. 

It acts as a standardized "question" code, often paired with SNOMED CT as the "answer".

## Key Aspects of LOINC Coding

Purpose: Created by the Regenstrief Institute in 1994, it facilitates data exchange and pools results for clinical care, research, and outcomes management.

Scope: Covers laboratory tests (chemistry, hematology, microbiology) and clinical measurements like vital signs, ECG, and survey instruments.

Structure: Each code is a unique, permanent number consisting of six axes: Analyte, Imaging modality, Time, Specimen, Scale, and Method.

Adoption: Used in over 170 countries and required by US federal systems for interoperability.

## LOINC vs. Other Coding Systems

LOINC: Identifies the observation or test name (e.g., Glucose [m/v] in blood).

SNOMED CT: Identifies the result value or clinical finding (e.g., "High blood sugar").

CPT/HCPCS: Used primarily for billing and administrative claims.

## Implementation and Tools

RELMA (Regenstrief LOINC Mapping Assistant): A tool to map local laboratory test codes to LOINC codes.

Usage: Often required in EHR systems for laboratory data exchange, often associated with Meaningful Use requirements.

Availability: The standard is free, and you can explore the LOINC database and Wikipedia's overview for more information.
