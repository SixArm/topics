# Spacelabs

Spacelabs is a medical technology company specializing in patient monitoring systems, diagnostic cardiology, and clinical data management. Founded in 1958 and headquartered in Snoqualmie, Washington, the company originally developed telemetry systems for the U.S. space program before pivoting to healthcare. It operates as a subsidiary of OSI Systems.

Spacelabs designs and manufactures a range of monitoring solutions used in hospitals, ambulatory care, and clinical research settings. Its product portfolio includes bedside and transport patient monitors, central monitoring stations, ambulatory blood pressure monitors, and Holter monitoring systems for cardiac diagnostics. The company also provides clinical information systems that aggregate and analyze patient data across departments.

The cardiac diagnostics line is a core strength. Spacelabs offers Holter monitors and analysis software for detecting arrhythmias, evaluating heart rate variability, and assessing ST-segment changes over extended recording periods. Products such as the Eclipse series and the LifeScreen Pro support multi-lead ECG acquisition with algorithms tuned for artifact rejection and beat classification. These systems integrate with centralized review stations where clinicians can edit, annotate, and generate reports.

Spacelabs differentiates through its focus on data connectivity and interoperability. Its monitoring platforms support HL7 interfaces and integration with electronic health record systems, enabling continuous data flow from bedside devices to enterprise clinical databases. This connectivity supports both real-time clinical decision-making and retrospective analysis for quality improvement.

The company serves hospitals, cardiac rehabilitation centers, pharmaceutical trial sites, and research institutions. Its systems are designed for scalability, allowing facilities to expand monitoring capacity without replacing core infrastructure. Spacelabs maintains regulatory clearances including FDA 510(k) for its devices sold in the United States and CE marking for distribution in the European Union.
