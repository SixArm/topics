# Hierarchical Task Analysis (HTA)

Question: What is the primary purpose of Hierarchical Task Analysis (HTA)?

- [ ] To assign team members to specific roles within an organization
- [ ] To break down complex tasks into a hierarchical structure of subtasks, actions, and operations
- [ ] To calculate the time required to complete individual work items
- [ ] To measure employee performance against predefined benchmarks

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To break down complex tasks into a hierarchical structure of subtasks, actions, and operations</p>
  <p>HTA is a systematic method for decomposing complex tasks into hierarchical levels, enabling detailed understanding of task structure, goals, and dependencies. This decomposition helps with process evaluations, schedule planning, risk identification, training needs, and workflow optimizations.</p>
</details>
