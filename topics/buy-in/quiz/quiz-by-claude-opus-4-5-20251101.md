# Buy-in

Question: What is the key difference between buy-in and compliance in an organizational context?

- [ ] Buy-in requires financial investment while compliance does not
- [ ] Compliance involves stakeholder engagement while buy-in does not
- [ ] Buy-in implies genuine belief and support while compliance may lack true commitment
- [ ] Buy-in is only needed for technical projects while compliance applies to all decisions

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Buy-in implies genuine belief and support while compliance may lack true commitment</p>
  <p>Buy-in represents a genuine belief in the value and importance of a project or decision, creating shared ownership and commitment. Compliance, in contrast, means someone may follow a directive without actually supporting it, which can lead to resentment and resistance over time.
