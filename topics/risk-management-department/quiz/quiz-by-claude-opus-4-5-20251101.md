# Risk management department

Question: What is the primary goal of a risk management department in an organization?

- [ ] To maximize company profits through aggressive investment strategies
- [ ] To eliminate all risks completely from business operations
- [ ] To identify, assess, and mitigate risks that may affect the organization's ability to achieve its objectives
- [ ] To manage employee performance reviews and compensation

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To identify, assess, and mitigate risks that may affect the organization's ability to achieve its objectives</p>
  <p>The risk management department's core mission is to systematically identify potential risks (financial, operational, legal, regulatory, reputational), assess their probability and impact, and develop strategies to mitigate or manage them. This includes compliance oversight, insurance management, crisis planning, and employee trainingâ€”all aimed at protecting the organization's ability to achieve its goals, not eliminating all risk entirely.</p>
