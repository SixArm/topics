# The Hard Thing About Hard Things by Ben Horowitz

Question: According to Ben Horowitz, what is the central reality of running a startup that entrepreneurs must accept?

- [ ] Success comes from following proven frameworks and best practices from larger companies
- [ ] Running a startup is inherently difficult with no easy answers or silver bullets for success
- [ ] The key to success is avoiding all unpopular decisions to maintain team harmony
- [ ] Startups succeed primarily through securing sufficient venture capital funding

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Running a startup is inherently difficult with no easy answers or silver bullets for success</p>
  <p>One of the main themes of the book is that running a startup is inherently difficult and there are no easy answers or silver bullets for success. Horowitz emphasizes the importance of making hard decisions and taking risks, even if they are unpopular with employees or investors. This realistic perspective helps entrepreneurs prepare for the challenges ahead rather than expecting a smooth path to success.</p>
</details>
