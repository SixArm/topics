# Digital certificate

Question: What is the primary purpose of a digital certificate in a secure communication network?

- [ ] To encrypt all data transmitted between two parties using symmetric encryption
- [ ] To verify the identity of an entity and confirm ownership of a public key
- [ ] To store private keys securely on a remote server
- [ ] To replace passwords as the sole method of user authentication

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To verify the identity of an entity and confirm ownership of a public key</p>
  <p>A digital certificate serves as an electronic document that verifies the identity of a person, organization, or device and confirms ownership of a public key. It contains a digital signature from a trusted certificate authority (CA), enabling recipients to trust that the public key belongs to the claimed entity and has not been tampered with.</p>
</details>
