# Dark factory

A dark factory (or lights-out manufacturing) is a highly automated production or software pipeline that operates with little to no human intervention. It is called "dark" because, theoretically, machines don't need light to function, allowing facilities to run with the lights turned off.🏭 Physical ManufacturingIn traditional manufacturing, dark factories use artificial intelligence, robotics, and automated handling systems to build products around the clock.Real-world Examples: Companies like FANUC in Japan operate plants where robots build other robots, and major manufacturers in China use dark factories to scale electric vehicle and appliance production with only a fraction of their usual workforce.Benefits: Massive reductions in overhead costs, fewer human safety risks, and the ability to build facilities in remote locations rather than near labor hubs.💻 Software and AIThe term has recently been adapted to describe fully automated, AI-driven software development workflows.How it works: Instead of humans writing and reviewing every line of code, the system takes high-level goals and uses AI agents to write, test, debug, and ship code directly to production without human micro-management.Benefits: Speeds up development times and frees human engineers to focus solely on architecture, spec planning, and governance.
