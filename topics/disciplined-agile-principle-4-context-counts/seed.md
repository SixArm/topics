# Disciplined Agile principle 4: Context counts

"Context counts: Acknowledge that every team and organization is unique. Your 'Way of Working' (WoW) must reflect your specific environment and evolve over time."

Principle 4: Context Counts is the logical partner to Pragmatism. It is based on the observation that every team, every project, and every organization is a "unique snowflake" with its own set of challenges.

In Disciplined Agile, this means you must account for several scaling factors:

- Team Size: A team of 5 people communicates differently than a team of 50.
- Geographic Distribution: Being in the same room is different from being spread across three time zones.
- Organizational Complexity: Startups have different hurdles than 100-year-old banks with heavy regulatory requirements.
- Technical Debt: Working on a brand-new "greenfield" app is different from modifying a legacy mainframe system.

Because of this, DA doesn't give you a "recipe"; it gives you a toolkit. Instead of saying "Do X," DA asks "What is your context?" and then points you toward the strategies most likely to work for that specific scenario.
