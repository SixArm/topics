# Angel investor

Question: What do angel investors typically receive in exchange for providing funding to early-stage startups?

- [ ] A fixed interest rate on their investment with guaranteed monthly payments
- [ ] An equity stake in the company, and potentially additional rights such as a board seat
- [ ] Full ownership of the company until the investment is repaid
- [ ] A percentage of the company's revenue until the investment amount is recovered

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>An equity stake in the company, and potentially additional rights such as a board seat</p>
  <p>Angel investors provide capital to early-stage startups in exchange for equity ownership. Unlike lenders who receive interest payments, angel investors become partial owners of the company. They may also receive additional rights and privileges, such as a seat on the board of directors or the ability to veto certain decisions. This equity-based model means angel investing is high-risk, high-rewardâ€”investors profit only if the startup succeeds and increases in value.</p>
