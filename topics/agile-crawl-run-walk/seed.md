# Agile crawl, walk, run

In Agile, the Crawl, Walk, Run approach is a maturity model used to implement new processes, tools, or mindsets without overwhelming the team. It emphasizes that you cannot achieve "Agile excellence" overnight; you must master the basics before tackling complex scaling.

## The Stages of Agile Maturity

- Crawl: Focus is on customer discovery, transparency, making work visible.
- Walk: Focus is on workflow standardization, such as Definition of Done, improving estimation accuracy, and beginning to automate testing (UAT).
- Run: Focus is on optimization, such as continuous Delivery (DevOps integration), advanced metrics (Lead/Cycle time), and scaling Agile across multiple departments.

## Why This Matters for Agile Teams

Avoids "Agile Fatigue": Introducing too many changes at once (like forcing full DevOps automation on a team that hasn't mastered Sprints) often leads to burnout and a return to old habits.

Incremental Value: Just as Agile delivers software in increments, the "Crawl, Walk, Run" method delivers process improvements in increments.

Builds Psychological Safety: It allows teams to "fail small" while they are still in the Crawl phase, making it safer to experiment as they progress to Walk and Run.
