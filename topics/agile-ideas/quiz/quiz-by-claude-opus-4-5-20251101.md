# Agile ideas

Question: Which of the following best describes when agile methodologies may NOT be the ideal approach for a project?

- [ ] When the team wants to deliver working software frequently
- [ ] When requirements are expected to change throughout the project
- [ ] When the project has fixed, well-defined requirements with regulatory compliance needs and minimal expected changes
- [ ] When close collaboration with stakeholders is possible and encouraged

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>When the project has fixed, well-defined requirements with regulatory compliance needs and minimal expected changes</p>
  <p>Agile contraindications refer to situations where agile may not be the best fit. Projects with fixed, well-defined requirements, strict regulatory compliance needs, and minimal expected changes may benefit more from traditional methodologies like waterfall. Agile excels when requirements evolve, frequent delivery is valued, and stakeholder collaboration is strongâ€”but these same strengths become less relevant when requirements are stable and documentation-heavy compliance is mandatory.</p>
</details>
