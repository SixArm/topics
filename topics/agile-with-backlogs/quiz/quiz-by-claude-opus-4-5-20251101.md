# Agile with backlogs

Question: What is the primary purpose of backlog refinement (grooming) in agile methodology?

- [ ] To assign developers to specific sprint tasks
- [ ] To add detail, estimates, and acceptance criteria to backlog items
- [ ] To measure team velocity across multiple sprints
- [ ] To report project status to executive stakeholders

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To add detail, estimates, and acceptance criteria to backlog items</p>
  <p>Backlog refinement (also called grooming) is an ongoing collaborative process where the team works together to add detail, estimates, and acceptance criteria to backlog items. This ensures that upcoming work is well-understood and ready for sprint planning, enabling the team to make informed commitments during sprint planning sessions.</p>
</details>
