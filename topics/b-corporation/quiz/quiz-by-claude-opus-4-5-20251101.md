# B-Corporation

Question: What is the primary purpose of the B Impact Assessment that companies must complete to become B-Corp certified?

- [ ] To evaluate the company's stock market performance and shareholder returns
- [ ] To measure the company's impact on stakeholders including customers, employees, suppliers, and the environment
- [ ] To determine the company's eligibility for government tax incentives
- [ ] To assess the company's competitive positioning against industry rivals

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To measure the company's impact on stakeholders including customers, employees, suppliers, and the environment</p>
  <p>The B Impact Assessment is a comprehensive evaluation that covers governance, worker compensation and benefits, supply chain practices, and environmental sustainability. It measures how a company's operations affect all its stakeholders, which is central to the B-Corp certification process and distinguishes it from traditional business certifications that focus primarily on financial metrics.</p>
</details>
