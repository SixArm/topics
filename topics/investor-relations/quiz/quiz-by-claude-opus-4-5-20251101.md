# Investor relations

Question: What is the primary purpose of investor relations (IR) in a technology company?

- [ ] To develop new software products and manage technical infrastructure
- [ ] To recruit and onboard new engineering talent for the organization
- [ ] To strategically manage communication and relationships with investors, shareholders, and the financial community while maintaining transparency and maximizing shareholder value
- [ ] To handle customer support tickets and resolve user complaints

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To strategically manage communication and relationships with investors, shareholders, and the financial community while maintaining transparency and maximizing shareholder value</p>
  <p>Investor relations (IR) is the strategic management of communication between a company and its financial stakeholders. The IR team conveys financial performance, business strategy, and future prospects to investors while building trust and ensuring transparency. Key functions include financial reporting, relationship management with shareholders and analysts, shareholder services, business intelligence on market trends, and ensuring corporate governance compliance.</p>
</details>
