# Assets and liabilities

Question: What does the equity of a business represent?

- [ ] The total amount of current liabilities due within one year
- [ ] The sum of all tangible and intangible assets owned
- [ ] The difference between its assets and its liabilities
- [ ] The total economic value recorded as debits on a balance sheet

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>The difference between its assets and its liabilities</p>
  <p>Equity represents the residual value that belongs to the owners or shareholders of the business after subtracting all liabilities from all assets. This fundamental accounting equation (Assets - Liabilities = Equity) is essential for understanding a company's net worth and financial health.</p>
</details>
