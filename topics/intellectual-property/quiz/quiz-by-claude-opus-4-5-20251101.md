# Intellectual Property

Question: What is the primary purpose of intellectual property (IP) laws?

- [ ] To prevent all forms of copying regardless of the creator's wishes
- [ ] To make all creative works freely available to the public
- [ ] To encourage innovation and creativity by granting exclusive rights to creators
- [ ] To maximize government revenue through registration fees

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To encourage innovation and creativity by granting exclusive rights to creators</p>
  <p>IP laws exist to incentivize innovation and creativity by giving creators exclusive rights to control and profit from their work. This protection—which includes patents, trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets—allows inventors and creators to benefit from their investments in research and development, which in turn encourages further innovation.</p>
</details>
