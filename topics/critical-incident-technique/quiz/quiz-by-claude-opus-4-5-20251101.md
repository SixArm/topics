# Critical Incident Technique (CIT)

Question: What is the primary objective of the Critical Incident Technique (CIT)?

- [ ] To automate incident response workflows across distributed systems
- [ ] To extract valuable insights and actionable knowledge from real-life events for decision-making and process improvement
- [ ] To create standardized documentation templates for software bugs
- [ ] To measure system uptime and availability metrics

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To extract valuable insights and actionable knowledge from real-life events for decision-making and process improvement</p>
  <p>CIT is a qualitative research method focused on gathering detailed information about significant events to inform decision-making, problem-solving, training, or process improvement efforts. It involves defining critical incidents, collecting details, selecting representative cases, coding themes, clustering patterns, and interpreting findings to derive actionable insights.</p>
</details>
