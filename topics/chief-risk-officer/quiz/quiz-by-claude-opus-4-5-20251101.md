# Chief Risk Officer (CRO)

Question: What is the primary role of a Chief Risk Officer (CRO) in an organization?

- [ ] To maximize company profits through aggressive investment strategies
- [ ] To identify, analyze, and manage risks while developing mitigation strategies
- [ ] To handle all human resources and employee relations matters
- [ ] To oversee the organization's marketing and sales operations

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To identify, analyze, and manage risks while developing mitigation strategies</p>
  <p>The CRO is responsible for identifying and understanding the risks an organization faces—including financial, legal, operational, and reputational risks—and working with senior leaders to develop and implement strategies that minimize the negative impact of those risks on the organization.</p>
</details>
