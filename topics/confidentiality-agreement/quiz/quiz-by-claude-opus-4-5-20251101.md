# Confidentiality agreement

Question: What is the primary purpose of a confidentiality agreement (NDA)?

- [ ] To establish a partnership contract between two companies
- [ ] To define employee compensation and benefits
- [ ] To protect confidential or proprietary information shared between parties
- [ ] To outline the terms of a service-level agreement

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To protect confidential or proprietary information shared between parties</p>
  <p>A confidentiality agreement, also known as a non-disclosure agreement (NDA), is a legal document that establishes a confidential relationship between two or more parties specifically to protect confidential or proprietary information that is shared between them. This is essential for technology professionals who frequently deal with sensitive data, trade secrets, and proprietary systems during collaborations, partnerships, or employment transitions.</p>
</details>
