# Burndown chart

A burndown chart is an Agile project management tool that visually tracks remaining work against time, displaying a downward trend toward zero. It typically features time on the horizontal axis and remaining effort (hours or story points) on the vertical axis, helping teams monitor progress, spot delays, and manage scope creep.

## Key Components and Types

- Ideal Work Remaining Line: A straight line indicating the planned, steady pace of work completion.
- Actual Work Remaining Line: Shows the actual, fluctuating progress of tasks.

## Benefits of a Burndown Chart

- Predicts Completion: Allows teams to forecast when they will finish all the work.
- Identifies Bottlenecks: Shows if the team is behind schedule, enabling early adjustments.
- Monitors Scope Creep: Upward spikes in the line indicate that new work was added to the existing work.
