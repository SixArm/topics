# Holter monitor

A Holter monitor is a small, wearable device that continuously records your heart's electrical activity (electrocardiogram or ECG) for 24 to 48 hours or longer. It is used to detect irregular heartbeats (arrhythmias) that a standard, short-term ECG might miss. It helps doctors diagnose conditions like heart palpitations, dizziness, and fainting. 

Key Details About Holter Monitors:
- How it Works: Electrodes (small, sticky patches) are attached to your chest, which connect to a portable recorder that stores your heart rate and rhythm data.
- Daily Activity: You wear the device under your clothes while going about your normal daily activities.
- What to Avoid: Typically, you cannot bathe, shower, or swim while wearing the monitor because it must stay dry.
- Recording Symptoms: You will likely be asked to keep a diary of your activities and any symptoms (e.g., chest pain, palpitations) you feel, noting the exact time they occur.
- After the Test: You return the device to the doctor, who will analyze the data to understand the cause of your symptoms. 

Why a Holter Monitor is Used:
- To evaluate symptoms like palpitations, dizziness, or fainting.
- To see how well a heart medication or treatment is working.
- To check for arrhythmias (abnormal heart rhythms). 

Wearing a Holter monitor is painless and safe.
