# Agile and risk management

Agile risk management is an iterative and continuous process of identifying and addressing uncertainties throughout a project's lifecycle, rather than a single upfront phase. While traditional methods rely on fixed, heavy documentation, Agile embeds risk management directly into its daily practices and ceremonies.

## 1. Embedded Risk Mitigation in Agile Practices

Agile frameworks naturally de-risk projects through built-in feedback loops and transparency:

- Customer Interactions: Used to brainstorm immediate risks and determine if a team can confidently deliver planned work.
- Continuous Visibility: Risk indicators where the team highlights "impediments" or blockers that could impact their goals.
- Working Software: Frequent product demonstrations with customers minimize the risk of building the wrong product.
- Reflections: Allow teams to reflect on how risks are handled and how to improve their handling.
- Technical Spikes: Timeboxed investigations into specific technical uncertainties to reduce risk before full implementation begins.

## 2. Scaling Risk Management: The ROAM Model

For larger organizations, Agile often employs the ROAM model during planning sessions to categorize and handle high-level risks:

- Resolved: The risk is no longer a threat and requires no further action.
- Owned: A specific team member is assigned to manage the risk if it cannot be resolved immediately.
- Accepted: The team acknowledges the risk and builds contingencies, as it cannot be fully eliminated.
- Mitigated: A plan is developed to reduce the likelihood or potential impact of the risk.

## 3. Key Tools and Visuals

Teams use lightweight visual tools to maintain visibility of project health:

- Risk-Adjusted Priority Queue: Prioritizing user stories based on both business value and the level of associated risk.
- Risk Matrix: A simple grid (often 3x3 or 5x5) used to quickly score risks based on their probability and impact.
- RAID Logs: A centralized record of Risks, Assumptions, Issues, and Dependencies.
