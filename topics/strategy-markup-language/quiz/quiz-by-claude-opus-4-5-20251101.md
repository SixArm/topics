# Strategy Markup Language (StratML)

Question: What is the primary purpose of Strategy Markup Language (StratML)?

- [ ] To replace traditional word processors for writing business documents
- [ ] To provide a common framework for describing and sharing strategic plans in a structured, machine-readable format
- [ ] To create visual diagrams of organizational hierarchies
- [ ] To encrypt sensitive government communications

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To provide a common framework for describing and sharing strategic plans in a structured, machine-readable format</p>
  <p>StratML is an XML-based open standard developed to capture and convey strategic goals, objectives, actions, resources, and measures in a way that improves transparency, accountability, and collaboration. It was created by the U.S. government to standardize how federal agencies report on strategic plans and performance, making it easier to share and compare strategic information across organizations.</p>
</details>
