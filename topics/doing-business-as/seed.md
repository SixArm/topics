# Doing Business As (DBA)

A "Doing Business As" (DBA) name, also known as a trade, fictitious, or assumed name, allows individuals and companies to operate under a name different from their legal, registered name. It is essential for sole proprietors to use a business name, and for corporations/LLCs to launch new brands without forming a new entity. 

Key Details About DBAs:

- Purpose: Notifies the public of the actual owner of a business, serving as a consumer protection tool.

- Usage: Allows for separate banking, marketing, and, in some cases, distinct legal branding.

- Limitations: A DBA does not create a new, separate legal entity or provide liability protection (like an LLC). 

When to Use a DBA:

- Sole Proprietors: Using a name other than your personal name (e.g., "John Smith" operating as "JS Consulting").

- LLCs/Corporations: Operating a new, different line of business or marketing under a different brand.

- Banking: Opening a business bank account under a specific trade name.

Steps to File a DBA:

- Check Availability: Ensure the desired name is not already in use by searching state or local databases.

- File Registration: Complete and submit the required forms to the county clerk or secretary of state.

- Public Notice: Some jurisdictions require publishing a notice of the new name in a local newspaper.
