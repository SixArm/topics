# UI/UX design fails because

Question: What is a primary reason why UI/UX design fails when designers skip the initial discovery phase?

- [ ] Slow website performance and high bounce rates
- [ ] Inconsistent visual styles across different pages
- [ ] Designs that don't resonate with the target audience due to assumptions made without proper user research
- [ ] Lack of clear calls-to-action throughout the interface

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Designs that don't resonate with the target audience due to assumptions made without proper user research</p>
  <p>Poor user research is a foundational failure in UI/UX design. When designers skip proper research and analysis, they make assumptions about user needs, behaviors, and preferences. This leads to designs that fail to connect with the actual target audience. While performance issues, visual inconsistency, and unclear CTAs are all valid concerns, they are typically symptoms that can be identified and corrected through testingâ€”whereas building on flawed assumptions from the start creates fundamental misalignment between the product and its users.</p>
</details>
