# Affinity Estimation (a.k.a. Silent Grouping)

Affinity Estimation, often called Silent Grouping, is a fast relative sizing
technique used by agile teams to estimate large backlogs (often 50+ items) in
under an hour. Unlike Planning Poker, which focuses on deep discussion, Affinity
Estimation relies on visual comparison and team intuition.  

## The Step-by-Step Process## Step 1: Silent Relative Sizing

* The Set-up: Create a horizontal scale on a wall or digital board (like [Miro](https://miro.com/templates/affinity-diagram/) or [Mural](https://www.mural.co/)) with "Smaller" on the left and "Larger" on the right.  
* The Rule: Team members take user story cards and place them on the scale relative to others—without speaking.  
* The Logic: If Story A feels twice as hard as Story B, place it further to the right. If someone disagrees with a placement, they simply move the card. If a card moves back and forth more than twice, set it aside for discussion.  

## Step 2: Editing the Wall (Discussion)

* Once all items are placed, the "silence" ends. The team reviews the distribution together.
* Clarify: The Product Owner answers questions about stories that were set aside or moved frequently.
* Refine: Adjust placements based on these brief technical discussions.  

## Step 3: Placing Items in "Buckets"

* Superimpose size categories onto your horizontal scale. Common buckets include T-Shirt Sizes (XS, S, M, L, XL) or Fibonacci Numbers (1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13).
* Any story falling within a bucket's boundary takes that estimate.  

## When to Use It vs. Planning Poker

| Feature  | Affinity Estimation | Planning Poker |
|---|---|---|
| Best For | New projects, large backlogs (50+ items) | Small projects, sprint planning (2–10 items) |
| Speed | Extremely fast (e.g., 100 stories in 1 hour) | Slower (minutes per story) |
| Depth | High-level comparison | Deep dive into technical risks |
| Goal | Rough roadmap & release planning | Precise sprint commitment |

## Expert Tip: The Hybrid Approach

Many startups use Affinity Estimation for high-level quarterly planning to get a rough idea of scope, then use Planning Poker during bi-weekly sprint planning for the specific stories they are about to build.
