# Culture eats strategy for breakfast

Question: What is the core meaning of Peter Drucker's quote "Culture eats strategy for breakfast"?

- [ ] Strategy should always be developed before considering organizational culture
- [ ] Organizational culture is a more powerful force than strategy in achieving success
- [ ] Breakfast meetings are the best time to discuss corporate strategy
- [ ] Companies should prioritize strategic planning over employee engagement

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Organizational culture is a more powerful force than strategy in achieving success</p>
  <p>The quote emphasizes that no matter how well-crafted a strategy may be, it will not succeed if it is not supported by a strong and aligned organizational culture. Organizations must ensure their shared values, beliefs, attitudes, and behaviors support their strategic goals. This means fostering innovation, developing a sense of purpose, and creating transparency, collaboration, and accountability alongside any strategic initiatives.</p>
</details>
