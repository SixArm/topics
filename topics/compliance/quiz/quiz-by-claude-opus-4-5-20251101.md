# Compliance

Question: What is the primary reason compliance is considered a critical component for any organization?

- [ ] It guarantees increased revenue and market share
- [ ] It eliminates the need for internal audits
- [ ] Non-compliance can lead to legal, financial, and reputational risks
- [ ] It replaces the need for employee training programs

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Non-compliance can lead to legal, financial, and reputational risks</p>
  <p>Compliance is critical because failing to adhere to rules, regulations, and standards can expose an organization to serious consequences including penalties, fines, legal actions, loss of business opportunities, and damage to reputation and brand image. Understanding this risk-mitigation purpose helps technology professionals prioritize compliance in system design, data handling, and operational processes.</p>
</details>
