# Standup meeting

Question: What are the three questions each team member answers during a standup meeting?

- [ ] What skills do you need to develop? What training do you want? What certifications are you pursuing?
- [ ] What did you work on yesterday? What are you planning to work on today? What obstacles are preventing you from making progress?
- [ ] What is your current workload? What is your capacity? What is your availability?
- [ ] What features did you ship? What bugs did you fix? What code did you review?

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>What did you work on yesterday? What are you planning to work on today? What obstacles are preventing you from making progress?</p>
  <p>The standup meeting follows a specific format where each team member answers these three questions. This structure helps keep everyone informed about project progress, identifies potential roadblocks early, and provides opportunities for team members to collaborate and support one another. The format is designed to be brief and focused, encouraging transparency and open communication.</p>
</details>
