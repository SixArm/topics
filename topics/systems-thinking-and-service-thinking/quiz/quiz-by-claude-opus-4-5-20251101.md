# Systems thinking and service thinking

Question: What is a key principle shared by systems thinking and service thinking when diagnosing organizational problems?

- [ ] Problems should be addressed by setting stricter individual performance targets
- [ ] Standardized procedures are the best way to handle variation in customer needs
- [ ] Problems often stem from system design rather than individual failures
- [ ] Inspection and control are the primary drivers of service quality

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Problems often stem from system design rather than individual failures</p>
  <p>Both systems thinking and service thinking view work as interconnected systems rather than isolated processes. Systems thinking specifically recognizes that problems often stem from the system's design rather than individual failures, encouraging leaders to examine root causes and unintended consequences. This contrasts with traditional "command and control" management that relies on targets, standardization, and inspectionâ€”approaches that the Vanguard Method argues actually create waste and reduce performance in service environments.</p>
