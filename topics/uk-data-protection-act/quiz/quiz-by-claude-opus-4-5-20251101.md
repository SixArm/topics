# UK Data Protection Act (DPA)

Question: Under the UK Data Protection Act 2018, which organization has the authority to enforce data protection regulations and impose fines for non-compliance?

- [ ] The European Data Protection Board (EDPB)
- [ ] The Financial Conduct Authority (FCA)
- [ ] The Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)
- [ ] The National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC)

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>The Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)</p>
  <p>The UK Data Protection Act 2018 grants the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) the authority to enforce data protection regulations and impose fines for non-compliance. The ICO is the UK's independent supervisory authority for data protection, responsible for monitoring and enforcing the DPA, handling complaints, and providing guidance to organizations on their data protection obligations.</p>
</details>
