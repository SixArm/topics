# Indulgence versus restraint

Question: In Geert Hofstede's cultural dimensions framework, what distinguishes an indulgent culture from a restrained culture?

- [ ] Restrained cultures encourage freedom of speech and individual expression
- [ ] Indulgent cultures prioritize self-discipline and conformity to societal norms
- [ ] Indulgent cultures emphasize social order and structure over personal happiness
- [ ] Indulgent cultures allow greater acceptance of leisure, personal choice, and the pursuit of happiness

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Indulgent cultures allow greater acceptance of leisure, personal choice, and the pursuit of happiness</p>
  <p>According to Hofstede's framework, indulgent cultures tend to allow and encourage individuals to enjoy life, fulfill their desires, and seek happiness. They display greater acceptance of leisure, freedom of speech, personal choice, and individual expression. In contrast, restrained cultures emphasize self-control, suppression of gratification, and adherence to social norms, prioritizing modesty, self-discipline, and conformity over personal enjoyment.</p>
</details>
