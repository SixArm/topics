# Long-term orientation versus short-term orientation

Question: In Hofstede's cultural dimensions framework, which characteristic best distinguishes a long-term oriented culture from a short-term oriented culture?

- [ ] Greater emphasis on flexibility and living in the present moment
- [ ] Higher propensity for immediate gratification and quick results
- [ ] Focus on perseverance, thriftiness, and delayed gratification
- [ ] Prioritization of personal stability over tradition

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Focus on perseverance, thriftiness, and delayed gratification</p>
  <p>Long-term oriented cultures emphasize planning for the future, saving and investing (thriftiness), persistence, and a willingness to delay immediate rewards for greater future benefits. In contrast, short-term oriented cultures prioritize immediate needs, quick results, and present-moment satisfaction. </details>
