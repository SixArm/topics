# Agile crawl, walk, run

The crawl, walk, run approach is a maturity model for implementing new Agile processes, tools, or mindsets without overwhelming the team. It recognizes that you cannot achieve Agile excellence overnight. You must master the basics before tackling complex scaling, just as Agile itself delivers software in increments.

In the crawl phase, the team focuses on customer discovery, transparency, and making work visible. This is the foundation where teams learn to collaborate openly, establish basic rituals like standups and retrospectives, and begin thinking in terms of iterative delivery rather than big-bang releases.

In the walk phase, the team standardizes workflows. This includes establishing a Definition of Done, improving estimation accuracy, and beginning to automate testing. Teams at this stage have internalized Agile fundamentals and are ready to add rigor and repeatability to their practices.

In the run phase, the team pursues optimization. This means implementing continuous delivery with DevOps integration, tracking advanced metrics like lead time and cycle time, and scaling Agile across multiple departments or product lines.

This progression matters because introducing too many changes at once often leads to burnout and a retreat to old habits. A team that has not yet mastered sprint cadence will struggle if forced into full DevOps automation. The staged approach builds psychological safety by allowing teams to fail small during the crawl phase, making it safer to experiment as they advance. Each stage delivers measurable process improvements, creating momentum and confidence that sustains the transformation over time.
