# Gibson's Affordance Theory

Question: What is the core concept of Gibson's affordance theory in the context of user interface design?

- [ ] Affordances are internal mental models that users must learn through training
- [ ] Affordances require complex cognitive processing to interpret environmental cues
- [ ] Affordances are the perceived possibilities for action that an environment or object offers to an individual
- [ ] Affordances are purely aesthetic properties that make interfaces visually appealing

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Affordances are the perceived possibilities for action that an environment or object offers to an individual</p>
  <p>Gibson's affordance theory explains that affordances are opportunities for action directly perceived in the environment. For technology professionals, this means designing interfaces where interactive elements naturally communicate their functionalityâ€”a button should look clickable, a slider should look draggable. This direct perception approach eliminates the need for users to rely on complex reasoning or extensive training to understand how to interact with a system.</p>
