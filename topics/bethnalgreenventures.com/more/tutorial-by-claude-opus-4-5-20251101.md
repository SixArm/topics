## Bethnal Green Ventures (BGV)

Bethnal Green Ventures (BGV) is a pioneering early-stage venture capital fund and accelerator headquartered in London, United Kingdom. Founded in 2012, BGV occupies a distinctive position in the European startup ecosystem by exclusively backing technology ventures that generate measurable social and environmental impact alongside financial returns.

## Mission and Philosophy

BGV operates on the principle that technology can be a powerful force for addressing systemic societal challenges. The organization rejects the false dichotomy between profit and purpose, demonstrating through its portfolio that mission-driven startups can achieve commercial success while creating meaningful change.

The fund targets founders building solutions for problems that affect communities, the environment, and underserved populations. This approach reflects the broader tech-for-good movement that has gained momentum across Europe and globally.

## The Accelerator Program

BGV runs a structured three-month accelerator program that provides comprehensive support to selected cohorts of startups.

**Program Components:**

- **Seed Funding**: Initial capital investment to cover early-stage operational costs including product development, market validation, and initial team building
- **Mentorship**: Access to experienced entrepreneurs, domain experts, and industry veterans who provide guidance on strategy, product, and growth
- **Workshops**: Structured curriculum covering essential startup skills from fundraising to impact measurement
- **Networking**: Introductions to potential investors, partners, customers, and other stakeholders in the impact ecosystem
- **Co-working Space**: Physical workspace in East London during the program duration
- **Demo Day**: Culminating pitch event to investors and the broader tech community

## Impact Measurement Approach

A distinguishing characteristic of BGV is its emphasis on rigorous impact assessment. Portfolio companies are expected to integrate impact measurement into their core operations rather than treating it as an afterthought.

**Key Impact Practices:**

- Establishing baseline metrics for the social or environmental problem being addressed
- Defining clear theories of change that connect product features to intended outcomes
- Tracking quantitative and qualitative impact indicators alongside business KPIs
- Reporting on impact progress to stakeholders including investors and the public

This discipline helps founders articulate their value proposition to mission-aligned investors and customers while maintaining accountability to their stated social mission.

## Geographic and Market Context

Based in Bethnal Green, East London, BGV is part of the broader London tech ecosystem while maintaining a distinct identity focused on purpose-driven ventures. The fund has contributed to establishing London and the UK as a hub for impact investing and social enterprise, influencing the development of similar programs across Europe.

## Key Takeaways

- BGV is a UK-based accelerator and VC fund exclusively focused on tech-for-good startups
- The three-month program provides funding, mentorship, and structured support
- Impact measurement is integral to the BGV model, not optional
- Portfolio includes successful ventures across health, climate, and civic technology
- The organization serves as a connector between mission-driven founders and impact-aligned capital
