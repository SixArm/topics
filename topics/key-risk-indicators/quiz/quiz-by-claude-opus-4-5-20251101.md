# Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)

Question: What is the primary purpose of Key Risk Indicators (KRIs) in an organization?

- [ ] To replace Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) entirely
- [ ] To provide an early warning of potential risks and identify negative trends
- [ ] To measure employee satisfaction levels
- [ ] To calculate annual revenue projections

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To provide an early warning of potential risks and identify negative trends</p>
  <p>KRIs are metrics that assess the level of risk of processes and provide an early warning of potential risks. They help identify trends that could have a negative impact on the organization, enabling senior management to monitor the overall risk profile and take timely action to mitigate risks before they escalate.</p>
</details>
