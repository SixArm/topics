# Runway

A startup funding runway is how long a company can operate before running out of cash, calculated by dividing total cash by the net burn rate. 

A healthy target is typically 18–24 months. Longer is recommended if there's a need to navigate tougher fundraising markets and ensure milestones are met.


Why It Matters: Insufficient runway is a top cause of startup failure.

Fundraising Threshold: Start raising funds when 8–10 months of runway remain. 

How to Extend Your Runway:

- Reduce Burn Rate: Cut discretionary spending, such as marketing, or optimize software subscriptions.

- Increase Revenue: Boost sales or pivot to higher-margin products.

- Leverage Freelancers: Use contractors instead of full-time employees to reduce overhead.

- Secure Non-Dilutive Funding: Explore grants or venture debt to avoid giving up equity. 

Steps to Calculate:

- Determine Cash on Hand: Sum total cash and liquid assets.

- Calculate Monthly Expenses: Include salaries, rent, software, and overhead.

- Determine Net Burn: Subtract monthly revenue from monthly expenses.

- Divide: Divide Cash on Hand by Net Burn Rate.
