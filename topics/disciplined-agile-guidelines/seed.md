# Disciplined Agile guidelines

The Disciplined Agile Guidelines are the practical "guardrails" for your daily actions. While the Principles are your philosophy and the Promises are your commitments, the Guidelines help you navigate the complex decisions you face when refining your Way of Working (WoW).

- Validate Learnings: Don't just assume a new process or feature is working. Use data, feedback, and "Minimum Viable Products" (MVPs) to prove that you are actually delivering value.
- Apply Design Thinking: Put yourself in the customer’s shoes. Use empathy and iterative prototyping to ensure you are solving the right problem in a way that is actually usable.
- Attend to Relationships through the Value Stream: Value isn't created in a vacuum. Build strong bridges with people "upstream" (who give you work) and "downstream" (who receive your work) to ensure a smooth flow.
- Create Effective Environments: This goes beyond just having a nice office. It’s about having the right tools, automated testing, and cultural support to let the team focus on their craft without friction.
- Change Culture by Changing Flow: You can't just tell people to "be agile." If you change the way work moves and improve the results people see, the organizational culture will naturally shift to support those better outcomes.
- Create Semi-Autonomous Self-Organizing Teams: Give teams the authority to make their own decisions about how they work, while keeping them aligned with the organization's overall goals (Enterprise Awareness).
- Adopt Meant-for-Purpose Governance: Move away from heavy "command and control" oversight. Use "lightweight" governance that supports teams with clear goals and automated metrics rather than slow, manual approvals.
- Leverage and Enhance Organizational Assets: If someone else in the company has already built a great library or process, use it! Then, contribute your own improvements back so the whole enterprise gets smarter.
