# Disciplined Agile guidelines

The Disciplined Agile guidelines are practical guardrails for daily actions. While the Disciplined Agile principles define your philosophy and the promises define your commitments, the guidelines help you navigate complex decisions when refining your Way of Working (WoW).

Validate your learnings. Do not assume a new process or feature is working. Use data, feedback, and minimum viable products to prove you are delivering real value before scaling further.

Apply design thinking. Put yourself in the customer's position. Use empathy and iterative prototyping to ensure you are solving the right problem in a way that people can actually use.

Attend to relationships through the value stream. Value is not created in isolation. Build strong connections with people upstream who provide your work and downstream who receive it, ensuring smooth flow across the organization.

Create effective environments. Provide the right tools, automated testing, and cultural support so teams can focus on their craft without friction. This goes well beyond physical workspace.

Change culture by changing flow. You cannot mandate agility through declarations. When you change the way work moves and people see improved results, the organizational culture naturally shifts to support those better outcomes.

Create semi-autonomous self-organizing teams. Give teams authority to decide how they work, while keeping them aligned with organizational goals through enterprise awareness.

Adopt meant-for-purpose governance. Replace heavy command-and-control oversight with lightweight governance that supports teams through clear goals and automated metrics rather than slow manual approvals.

Leverage and enhance organizational assets. When someone in the company has already built a strong library or process, use it. Then contribute your improvements back so the entire enterprise benefits.
