# Accessible design

Question: What is the primary goal of accessible design (also known as inclusive design or universal design)?

- [ ] To create premium products specifically designed for users with disabilities
- [ ] To reduce development costs by simplifying user interfaces
- [ ] To create products and experiences usable by as many people as possible, regardless of abilities
- [ ] To comply with legal requirements while minimizing design changes

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To create products and experiences usable by as many people as possible, regardless of abilities</p>
  <p>Accessible design aims to remove barriers and ensure equal access and participation for individuals with diverse needs. It encompasses physical, visual, auditory, and cognitive accessibility, benefiting not only individuals with disabilities but providing a better user experience for all users. This approach promotes equal participation, independence, and empowerment across society.</p>
</details>
