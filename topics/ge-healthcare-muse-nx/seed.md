# GE HealthCare MUSE NX

The MUSE system (currently available as MUSE NX) is GE HealthCare’s flagship cardiology information management system. It serves as a central hub that integrates cardiac data from various devices—such as ECGs, stress tests, and Holter monitors—directly into a hospital's Electronic Medical Record (EMR).

Core Capabilities

- Centralised Data Management: Provides a single access point for a patient's complete longitudinal cardiology record, allowing clinicians to compare current and previous ECGs on one screen.
- Advanced Clinical Algorithms: Utilises the industry-standard Marquette 12SL ECG analysis program to provide validated measurements and serial comparisons, which can reduce false-positive admissions for chest pain by up to 50%.
- Open Integration: While previously limited to GE devices, newer versions (v9 and NX) support DICOM and HL7 protocols, enabling data import from third-party manufacturers and wearable devices.
- Remote Access: Physicians can review and edit cardiac reports from anywhere in the hospital or remotely via web-client solutions and mobile devices like iPads.

Key Benefits

- Faster Treatment: Implementation has been shown to reduce "door-to-balloon" times for heart attack patients by up to 30% by allowing paramedics to transmit ECGs directly from ambulances to the ER.
- Home-to-Hospital Care: Integrates with personal ECG devices like AliveCor’s KardiaMobile 6L, allowing medical-grade data from home to flow directly into the clinical workflow.

Market Presence

- High Adoption: MUSE is used by 9 out of 10 top-ranked cardiology and heart surgery hospitals in the U.S..
- Scalability: Available in different tiers, including MUSE EHX for smaller single facilities and MUSE NX for large multi-facility enterprises.
