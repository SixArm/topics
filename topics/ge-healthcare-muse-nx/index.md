# GE HealthCare MUSE NX

GE HealthCare MUSE NX is the company's flagship cardiology information management system, serving as a centralized hub that integrates cardiac data from ECGs, stress tests, and Holter monitors directly into a hospital's Electronic Medical Record (EMR). It provides a single access point for a patient's complete longitudinal cardiology record, enabling clinicians to compare current and historical ECGs on one screen.

MUSE NX uses the Marquette 12SL ECG analysis program, an industry-standard algorithm suite that delivers validated measurements and serial comparisons. This analytical capability can reduce false-positive admissions for chest pain by up to 50%, directly improving both clinical accuracy and resource utilization.

Integration is a central strength. While earlier MUSE versions were limited to GE devices, MUSE NX supports DICOM and HL7 protocols, enabling data import from third-party manufacturers and consumer wearable devices. For example, it integrates with AliveCor's KardiaMobile 6L, allowing medical-grade ECG data captured at home to flow directly into the clinical workflow. Physicians can review and edit cardiac reports remotely via web clients and mobile devices.

The system delivers measurable clinical impact. Hospitals using MUSE have reduced door-to-balloon times for heart attack patients by up to 30%, largely because paramedics can transmit ECGs from ambulances directly to the emergency department for immediate cardiologist review.

MUSE holds dominant market share: 9 out of 10 top-ranked U.S. cardiology and heart surgery hospitals use the platform. GE offers tiered deployments, with MUSE EHX targeting smaller single-site facilities and MUSE NX designed for large multi-facility enterprises requiring centralized data management at scale.
