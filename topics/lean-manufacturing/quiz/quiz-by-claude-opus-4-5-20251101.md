# Lean manufacturing

Question: What is the primary goal of Lean manufacturing?

- [ ] Maximizing production volume regardless of customer demand
- [ ] Building large inventory buffers to ensure product availability
- [ ] Maximizing value for customers while minimizing waste
- [ ] Implementing complex forecasting systems for production planning

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Maximizing value for customers while minimizing waste</p>
  <p>Lean manufacturing is fundamentally a production philosophy aimed at maximizing value from the customer's perspective while systematically eliminating waste (Muda) and inefficiencies. This is achieved through practices like just-in-time pull systems, continuous improvement (Kaizen), value stream mapping, and optimizing flowâ€”all centered on delivering what customers actually want without excess inventory, overproduction, or non-value-adding activities.</p>
</details>
