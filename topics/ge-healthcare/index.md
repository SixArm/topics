# GE HealthCare

GE HealthCare is a global medical technology and digital solutions company headquartered in Chicago, Illinois. Originally the healthcare division of General Electric, it became an independent, publicly traded company in January 2023. It serves over one billion patients annually across more than 100 countries, with approximately $20.6 billion in revenue for 2025 and roughly $1 billion invested annually in research and development.

The company operates through four primary segments. Imaging provides advanced diagnostic systems including CT, MRI, molecular imaging, and X-ray technologies. Ultrasound offers specialized systems for radiology, cardiovascular, women's health, and handheld point-of-care applications. Patient Care Solutions focuses on medical devices and digital tools for patient monitoring, diagnostic cardiology, anesthesia, and maternal-infant care. Pharmaceutical Diagnostics develops contrast agents and radiopharmaceuticals used to enhance medical imaging procedures.

GE HealthCare has pursued growth through strategic acquisitions, agreeing in November 2025 to acquire Intelerad Medical Systems for $2.3 billion to strengthen its medical imaging software portfolio, and acquiring Intelligent Ultrasound's clinical AI business in July 2024. The company has also invested heavily in AI-enabled products through its Edison intelligence platform, designed to help clinicians make faster and more precise diagnostic decisions.

On the hardware innovation front, GE HealthCare introduced Freelium in March 2025, a sealed magnet platform for MRI systems that uses less than one percent of the helium required by traditional systems. This addresses a significant supply chain constraint, improving MRI accessibility in regions with limited helium availability. The combination of acquisitions, AI integration, and sustainable engineering positions GE HealthCare as a major force in the convergence of medical devices, imaging software, and clinical decision support.
