# GE HealthCare 

GE HealthCare is a global medical technology and digital solutions innovator headquartered in Chicago, Illinois. Originally the healthcare division of the American conglomerate General Electric, it became an independent, publicly traded company on 4 January 2023. It serves over 1 billion patients annually across more than 100 countries. 

## Core Business Divisions

The company operates through four primary segments: 

- Imaging: Provides advanced diagnostic systems including CT, MRI, Molecular Imaging, and X-ray technologies.
- Ultrasound: Offers specialized systems for radiology, cardiovascular, women's health (Voluson), and handheld point-of-care (Vscan Air) applications.
- Patient Care Solutions: Focuses on medical devices and digital tools for patient monitoring, diagnostic cardiology, anesthesia, and maternal-infant care.
- Pharmaceutical Diagnostics: Develops and manufactures contrast agents and radiopharmaceuticals used to enhance medical imaging procedures. 

## Recent Developments (2024–2026)

- Expansion through Acquisitions: In November 2025, GE HealthCare agreed to acquire Intelerad Medical Systems for $2.3 billion to strengthen its medical imaging software portfolio. It also acquired Intelligent Ultrasound's clinical AI business in July 2024.
- Sustainable Innovation: In March 2025, the company introduced Freelium, a sealed magnet platform for MRIs that uses less than 1% of the helium required by traditional systems, improving accessibility in regions with limited helium supply.
- AI Integration: The company has heavily invested in AI-enabled products, such as its Edison intelligence platform, to help clinicians make faster and more precise decisions. 

## Key Figures

- Revenue: Reported approximately $20.6 billion for 2025.
- Investment: Annually invests roughly $1 billion in research and development.
