# Masculinity versus femininity

Question: In Hofstede's cultural dimensions, what is the primary distinction between masculine and feminine cultures in a workplace context?

- [ ] Masculine cultures prioritize work-life balance while feminine cultures emphasize career achievement
- [ ] Feminine cultures value cooperation and quality of life while masculine cultures emphasize competition and achievement
- [ ] Masculine cultures focus on community well-being while feminine cultures value individual success
- [ ] Feminine cultures have more distinct gender roles while masculine cultures have fluid role definitions

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Feminine cultures value cooperation and quality of life while masculine cultures emphasize competition and achievement</p>
  <p>According to Hofstede's cultural dimension framework, masculine cultures emphasize assertiveness, competition, achievement, and material success, while feminine cultures prioritize cooperation, nurturing, harmonious relationships, and quality of life. This distinction affects workplace dynamics, with masculine cultures often prioritizing career success and status, while feminine cultures tend to value work-life balance and collective well-being.</p>
</details>
