# Agile-adjacent ceremonies

Agile-adjacent ceremonies are structured rituals drawn from methodologies like Lean Startup, Shape Up, and Design Thinking. While they share the iterative spirit of standard Agile meetings such as Sprint Planning or Retrospectives, they emphasize strategy, validation, and deep problem-shaping rather than sprint-level execution.

Lean Startup rituals focus on reducing business risk through experimentation. The Build-Measure-Learn loop is a continuous operational practice where teams test specific business hypotheses with a minimum viable product. The Pivot or Persevere meeting is a strategic session held after reviewing experimental data, where the team decides whether to continue the current strategy or shift to a new hypothesis. Innovation Accounting provides regular check-ins focused on learning milestones rather than traditional metrics like revenue or user growth.

Shape Up, developed by Basecamp, replaces daily stand-ups with more intentional, lower-frequency rituals. The Betting Table is a leadership meeting held at the end of a cool-down period to decide which shaped pitches to commit to for the next six-week cycle. The Pitch Review has senior members present a document outlining a problem, a proposed solution, and its boundaries. The Cool-Down Period is a two-week interval between cycles with no scheduled meetings or new features, reserved for bug fixing and free-form exploration.

Design Thinking workshops handle discovery before implementation begins. Empathy Mapping Sessions are collaborative workshops that deep-dive into user personas and their emotional states. The Design Sprint is a compressed five-day process for answering critical business questions through design, prototyping, and customer testing. Solution Storyboarding has the team visually map the ideal user journey for a new feature.

These ceremonies complement standard Agile practices by addressing the strategic and discovery gaps that sprint-based frameworks often leave open.
