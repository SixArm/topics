# Agile-adjacent ceremonies

"Agile-adjacent" ceremonies are rituals that bring structure to methodologies like Lean Startup, Shape Up, and Design Thinking. While they mirror the iterative nature of standard Agile meetings (like Sprint Planning or Retrospectives), they focus more on strategy, validation, and deep problem-shaping.

## 1. Lean Startup Rituals

These ceremonies focus on reducing business risk and accelerating learning through experimentation.

- The Build-Measure-Learn Loop: Not a meeting, but a continuous operational ritual where teams verify a specific business hypothesis with an MVP.
- "Pivot or Persevere" Meeting: A strategic session held after reviewing experimental data. The team must decide: do we continue with our current strategy or shift to a new hypothesis?.
- Innovation Accounting: A regular check-in focused on "learning milestones" rather than just traditional metrics like revenue or user growth.

## 2. Shape Up Ceremonies

Basecamp’s [Shape Up methodology](https://basecamp.com/shapeup) replaces daily stand-ups with more intentional, lower-frequency rituals.

- The Betting Table: A high-level meeting at the end of a "cool-down" period where leaders decide which "shaped" pitches to commit to for the next six-week cycle.
- Pitch Review: Senior members present a "pitch"—a document outlining a problem, a potential solution, and its boundaries—to ensure it is ready for the dev team.
- Cool-Down Period: A two-week "sacred" time between cycles with no scheduled meetings or new features, used for bug fixing and free-form exploration.

## 3. Design Thinking Workshops

These ceremonies are used for "discovery" before a single line of code is written.

- Empathy Mapping Session: A collaborative workshop to deep-dive into user personas and their emotional state.
- The Design Sprint: A compressed, five-day process for answering critical business questions through design, prototyping, and testing ideas with customers.
- Solution Storyboarding: A group ritual where the team visually maps out the "ideal" journey a user would take with a new feature.

## Summary of Ritual Cadence

| Ceremony           | Methodology     | Frequency              | Core Objective                  |
| ------------------ | --------------- | ---------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Betting Table      | Shape Up        | Every 8 weeks          | Deciding which projects to fund |
| Pivot or Persevere | Lean Startup    | As needed (post-test)  | Choosing strategic direction    |
| Design Sprint      | Design Thinking | Once per major feature | Rapid prototyping & validation  |
| Cool-Down          | Shape Up        | Every 6 weeks          | Maintenance & mental reset      |
