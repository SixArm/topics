# Agile and user acceptance testing (UAT)

In Agile, user acceptance testing (UAT) is a continuous process integrated into each sprint rather than a final phase, ensuring software meets business needs early. It focuses on validating user stories against defined acceptance criteria, with product owners or end-users testing functionality. This approach reduces defects and ensures high-quality, relevant software. 

Key Aspects of UAT in Agile:

- Continuous Testing: Unlike Waterfall, where UAT happens at the end, Agile UAT takes place during each sprint, providing immediate feedback. 

- Acceptance Criteria: Before development, specific, measurable criteria are defined for each user story. These criteria act as the basis for UAT. 

- Active Involvement: Product owners or business users are involved throughout the process, verifying that the delivered feature matches their needs. 

- Reduced Risk: Early and regular user feedback allows for corrections during development, reducing the cost and time of fixing issues post-release. 

## Best Practices for Agile UAT:

- Plan Ahead: Plan UAT sessions in advance to avoid last-minute, rushed testing. 

- Empower Product Owners: They act as the voice of the customer and final authority on acceptance. 

- Incorporate Feedback Immediately: When a feature does not meet criteria, feedback is brought back into the sprint backlog for quick remediation. 

- Automate Where Possible: While UAT is often manual, automating tests for objective criteria ensures faster feedback, especially for regression testing. 

## Differences in Agile vs. Traditional UAT:

- Timing: Continuous/Sprint-based vs. Final project phase.
- Scope: Focused on specific, small functionality vs. Entire system.
- Collaboration: High involvement of stakeholders throughout vs. Primarily at the end.
