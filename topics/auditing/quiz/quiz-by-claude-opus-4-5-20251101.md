# Auditing

Question: What is the primary purpose of auditing in information security?

- [ ] To automate the deployment of software updates across systems
- [ ] To detect unauthorized access or suspicious activity by reviewing system logs and data
- [ ] To encrypt sensitive data during transmission over networks
- [ ] To manage user passwords and authentication credentials

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To detect unauthorized access or suspicious activity by reviewing system logs and data</p>
  <p>Auditing involves the regular review and analysis of system activity logs to detect any unauthorized access or suspicious activity that may compromise the integrity, confidentiality, or availability of sensitive information. This includes collecting and analyzing data from system logs, network traffic, and user activity to identify patterns, trends, and anomalies that may indicate security incidents or policy violations.</p>
</details>
