# Agile-adjacent methodologies

"Agile-adjacent" methodologies are frameworks that share Agile’s core values of iteration and customer feedback but often solve specific problems where standard Scrum or Kanban might fail, such as long-term planning or deep user research.

## 1. Lean Startup

While Agile focuses on delivering software, [Lean Startup](https://productschool.com/blog/product-fundamentals/product-management-frameworks) focuses on validated learning.

- Core Concept: The Build-Measure-Learn loop.
- Key Tool: The Minimum Viable Product (MVP), used to test a business hypothesis with the least effort possible.
- Best For: Early-stage startups trying to find product-market fit rather than just managing a development team.

## 2. Shape Up

Created by Basecamp, [Shape Up](https://anasalort.medium.com/scrum-vs-kanban-vs-shape-up-d5ee554014da) addresses "sprint fatigue" by moving away from two-week cycles.

- The Cycle: Teams work in six-week cycles followed by a two-week "cool down" for fixing bugs and exploring new ideas.
- Shaping vs. Building: Work is "shaped" (roughly sketched out with boundaries) by senior members before it ever reaches the dev team to ensure it's feasible.
- Best For: Teams that feel standard Scrum is too meeting-heavy or prevents them from finishing meaningful, large features.

## 3. Design Thinking

This is a problem-solving framework that prioritises [human-centered design](https://www.castandhue.com/post/design-thinking-vs-agile-vs-lean) over technical requirements.

- The Five Stages: Empathise, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test.
- The Difference: Design Thinking focuses on solving the right problem, while Agile focuses on solving the problem right.
- Best For: The "Discovery" phase of a project when you aren't yet sure what the users actually need.

## 4. Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)

JTBD is a strategy framework that views products through the "job" a customer is trying to accomplish.

- Core Concept: Customers don't buy products; they "hire" them to make progress in a specific circumstance.
- Best For: Redefining your product roadmap based on user motivation rather than just a list of requested features.

| --------------- | ----------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Lean Startup    | Validate business ideas | Build-Measure-Learn Loop   |

| JTBD            | Understand motivation   | "Hiring" products for jobs |
