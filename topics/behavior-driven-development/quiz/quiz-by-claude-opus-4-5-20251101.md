# Behavior Driven Development (BDD)

Question: What distinguishes Behavior Driven Development (BDD) from Test Driven Development (TDD)?

- [ ] BDD uses compiled languages while TDD uses interpreted languages
- [ ] BDD focuses on behavior specification and collaboration with business stakeholders
- [ ] BDD eliminates the need for automated testing
- [ ] BDD requires developers to work in isolation from other team members

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>BDD focuses on behavior specification and collaboration with business stakeholders</p>
  <p>BDD is an extension of TDD that shifts emphasis from unit testing to behavior specification and documentation. It emphasizes collaboration between developers, testers, and business stakeholders to create a shared understanding of project goals, ensuring the delivered software meets business requirements.</p>
</details>
