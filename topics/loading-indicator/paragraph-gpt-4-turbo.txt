A loading indicator is a UI/UX component that visually communicates to users
that a system or application is processing, loading content, or completing a
task in the background. Commonly represented as spinners, progress bars, dots,
or animations, loading indicators help manage user expectations by providing
feedback that the system is actively working and hasn’t frozen. They play a
crucial role in maintaining engagement and reducing frustration during wait
times, especially for actions like data fetching, file uploads, or page
transitions. A well-designed loading indicator enhances the overall user
experience by signaling responsiveness and keeping users informed during delays.
