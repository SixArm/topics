# Ability, capability, capacity

Question: What is the key distinction between ability, capability, and capacity in organizational contexts?

- [ ] Ability refers to maximum output volume, capability refers to innate talent, and capacity refers to available tools
- [ ] Ability refers to skill or talent for specific tasks, capability encompasses broader skills and resources for achieving outcomes, and capacity refers to the maximum potential or workload that can be handled
- [ ] All three terms are synonymous and can be used interchangeably without any meaningful difference
- [ ] Capacity is an individual attribute, ability is organizational, and capability measures financial resources

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>Ability refers to skill or talent for specific tasks, capability encompasses broader skills and resources for achieving outcomes, and capacity refers to the maximum potential or workload that can be handled</p>
  <p>Ability refers to inherent or acquired skill to perform a specific task. Capability is broader, combining skills, expertise, resources, tools, and processes to achieve outcomes. Capacity represents the maximum limit or potential for performance within a timeframe, influenced by workforce, infrastructure, and resources. Understanding these distinctions helps technology professionals accurately assess and communicate what individuals and organizations can accomplish.</p>
</details>
