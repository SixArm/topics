# Master of Business Administration (MBA)

Question: What is the primary purpose of a Master of Business Administration (MBA) degree?

- [ ] To provide specialized training exclusively in software development methodologies
- [ ] To certify professionals as licensed accountants or financial auditors
- [ ] To equip students with the knowledge and skills to manage a business or organization effectively
- [ ] To prepare students solely for academic research careers in economics

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To equip students with the knowledge and skills to manage a business or organization effectively</p>
  <p>An MBA is a graduate degree designed to provide broad business education across areas like finance, accounting, marketing, operations, and organizational behavior. For technology professionals, an MBA can open doors to executive leadership positions, management consulting, and entrepreneurship by combining technical expertise with business acumen.</p>
</details>
