# Usability friction

Question: What is the primary goal of reducing usability friction in product design?

- [ ] To increase the number of features available to users
- [ ] To improve user satisfaction, engagement, and overall product performance
- [ ] To add more security checkpoints throughout the user journey
- [ ] To ensure the product works only on the latest devices

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To improve user satisfaction, engagement, and overall product performance</p>
  <p>Usability friction refers to resistance or obstacles users encounter when interacting with a product. Reducing friction is a key goal in product design because it leads to improved user satisfaction, increased engagement, and better overall product performance. This involves addressing common friction areas such as complicated UIs, slow loading times, complex onboarding, confusing navigation, lengthy forms, inadequate error messages, cumbersome payment processes, inaccessibility, poor mobile responsiveness, unnecessary steps, and information overload.</p>
</details>
