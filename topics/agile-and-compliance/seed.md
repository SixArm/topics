# Agile and compliance

In Agile development, compliance is often viewed as a "gate" at the end of a project, but successful Agile teams treat it as a continuous design constraint integrated into every sprint. 

## The Challenge of Agile Compliance

Traditional compliance relies on fixed requirements and static documentation, which can clash with Agile's iterative nature. Common friction points include: 

- Documentation Gaps: Agile prioritises "working software over comprehensive documentation," while auditors require detailed audit trails.
- Approval Delays: Sequential sign-off processes can stall rapid release cycles.
- Terminology Gaps: Engineers and legal teams often define terms like "test" differently, leading to miscommunication. 

## Core Strategies for Success

To balance speed with regulatory safety, organisations use several key strategies:

- "Shift Left" Compliance: Involve legal and risk experts early in planning and backlog refinement rather than as external gatekeepers at the end. 

- Definition of Done (DoD): Include specific regulatory requirements in the team's "Definition of Done" so no feature is considered complete until it meets compliance standards. 

- Compliance as Code: Codify policies into automated tests that run within CI/CD pipelines, providing real-time validation and automated audit trails. 

- Agile Governance: Implement lightweight milestones and metrics (like the Disciplined Agile (DA) toolkit) to provide the governance regulators expect without the heavy paperwork of waterfall.
