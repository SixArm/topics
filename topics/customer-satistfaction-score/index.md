# Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT)

Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT) is a key performance indicator that measures how satisfied customers are with a specific product, service, or interaction. It typically uses a 1-to-5 scale ranging from "very dissatisfied" to "very satisfied." The score is calculated as the percentage of respondents who selected the top satisfaction ratings (usually 4 or 5) divided by the total number of responses.

CSAT surveys are usually deployed immediately after a specific interaction, such as a support call, a purchase, or a feature onboarding flow. This proximity to the event captures sentiment while the experience is fresh, making the data actionable for the team responsible. Common survey questions include "How would you rate your overall satisfaction with the service you received?" or "How satisfied were you with the support provided?"

A higher CSAT percentage indicates greater satisfaction, with 100% representing a perfect score. However, the metric has known limitations. Response rates are often low, typically around 5%, and respondents tend to skew toward extremes, being either very satisfied or very dissatisfied. This self-selection bias means CSAT is best used as a directional signal rather than an absolute measure.

To improve CSAT, teams should reduce customer effort by streamlining resolution paths, personalize interactions using available customer data, act on feedback systematically through monitoring platforms, and set clear expectations about product capabilities and response times.

CSAT differs from related metrics in scope. It measures short-term satisfaction with a specific interaction, whereas Net Promoter Score (NPS) gauges long-term loyalty and likelihood to recommend, and Customer Effort Score (CES) measures the ease of completing a task or resolving an issue.
