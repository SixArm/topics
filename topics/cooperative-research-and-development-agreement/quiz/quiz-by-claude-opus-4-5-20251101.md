# Cooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA)

Question: What is the primary purpose of a Cooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA)?

- [ ] To allow government agencies to outsource all research activities to private companies
- [ ] To encourage collaboration between government and external organizations to promote scientific and technological advancements in the public interest
- [ ] To provide exclusive licensing rights for government patents to the highest bidder
- [ ] To replace traditional government procurement contracts for technology purchases

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To encourage collaboration between government and external organizations to promote scientific and technological advancements in the public interest</p>
  <p>A CRADA is a legal agreement between a government agency or laboratory and external organizations (industry, academia, or other government agencies) specifically designed to promote collaboration that advances scientific and technological knowledge in the public interest. Key benefits include providing external organizations access to government facilities, expertise, and resources, as well as creating a streamlined mechanism for transferring government-developed technology to the private sector.</p>
</details>
