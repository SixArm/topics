# Decision record

Question: What is the primary purpose of a decision record (DR)?

- [ ] To assign blame when decisions lead to negative outcomes
- [ ] To replace verbal communication during team meetings
- [ ] To document and communicate decisions, their rationale, and associated actions for transparency and future reference
- [ ] To create a legal contract between stakeholders

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To document and communicate decisions, their rationale, and associated actions for transparency and future reference</p>
  <p>A decision record captures the decision-making process, the rationale behind the decision, and any associated actions or next steps. Its purpose is to provide transparency, accountability, documentation for future reference, clarity among stakeholders, and consistency in how similar decisions are made over time. DRs are commonly used in project management and software development environments where decisions involve multiple people.</p>
</details>
