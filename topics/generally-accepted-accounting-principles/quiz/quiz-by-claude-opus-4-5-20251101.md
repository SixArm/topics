# Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP)

Question: What is the primary purpose of Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP)?

- [ ] To replace International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) globally
- [ ] To eliminate the need for financial audits
- [ ] To ensure financial statements are accurate, transparent, and comparable across organizations
- [ ] To simplify accounting by removing disclosure requirements

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To ensure financial statements are accurate, transparent, and comparable across organizations</p>
  <p>GAAP provides a standardized set of guidelines, standards, and rules that govern accounting and financial reporting. This standardization ensures that financial statements from different organizations can be reliably compared, that the information presented is accurate, and that stakeholders have transparency into a company's financial health. For technology professionals, understanding GAAP is important when building financial systems, interpreting company financials, or working with accounting software.</p>
</details>
