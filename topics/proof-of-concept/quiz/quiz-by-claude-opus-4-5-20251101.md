# Proof of Concept (POC)

Question: What is the primary purpose of a proof of concept (POC) in a business context?

- [ ] To finalize the marketing strategy for a product launch
- [ ] To verify that an idea or concept will work in practice before investing significant resources
- [ ] To replace the need for user testing after product development
- [ ] To document all technical specifications for regulatory compliance

<details>
  <summary>Answer</summary>
  <p>To verify that an idea or concept will work in practice before investing significant resources</p>
  <p>A proof of concept is designed to demonstrate feasibility and gather feedback from stakeholders before committing substantial resources to full development. This reduces risk, increases confidence in the viability of the concept, and helps inform decision-making by identifying potential issues early in the process.</p>
</details>
