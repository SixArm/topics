# AliveCor

AliveCor is a medical device company specializing in personal electrocardiography (ECG) technology. Founded in 2010 and headquartered in Mountain View, California, the company develops FDA-cleared portable ECG devices that enable individuals and clinicians to capture medical-grade heart rhythm data outside traditional clinical settings.

AliveCor's flagship products are the KardiaMobile and KardiaMobile 6L, compact sensors that pair with a smartphone app to record single-lead and six-lead ECGs respectively. Users place their fingers on the device's electrodes for 30 seconds to capture a recording. The companion Kardia app uses machine learning algorithms to analyze the tracing and detect conditions such as atrial fibrillation, bradycardia, tachycardia, and normal sinus rhythm. The company also offers the Kardia 12L, which captures a full 12-lead ECG comparable to what a hospital would produce.

From a technology standpoint, AliveCor's platform combines analog front-end signal acquisition, ultrasonic data transmission (in earlier models) or Bluetooth Low Energy, and cloud-based AI interpretation. Their deterministic neural network models are FDA-cleared as software as a medical device (SaMD), placing them in a regulated AI category that requires clinical validation and ongoing post-market surveillance.

AliveCor operates a subscription service called KardiaCare, which provides unlimited ECG storage, monthly heart health reports, and medication tracking. This recurring revenue model complements hardware sales and generates a longitudinal dataset valuable for population health analytics.

The company holds a significant patent portfolio covering smartphone-based ECG acquisition, which has been the subject of litigation with Apple over the Apple Watch's ECG feature. AliveCor's technology has been used in numerous clinical studies and is integrated into telehealth workflows, making it a key player in the remote cardiac monitoring market.
